    Meru - A family from the Muthiani area has suffered a double tragedy after two sisters died in a period of two days.

    The two sisters, Fredah Kinanu Murugu and Kathleen Nkatha Murugu, died within 48 hours of each other in Migori county.

    Friends and family are still reeling from shock following their deaths, as they looked healthy all through the festive season and were cheerful as always.

    "Both Kathy and Fredah did not have any underlying health issues, so that's why it's even a bigger shock to the family because they were both very healthy and were at the prime of their age," said a family spokesperson, John Rutere.

    How did the Murugu sisters die

    Rutere explained that Kathleen travelled to Migori, where she was attending a festival, but was found dead in her hotel room.

    He disclosed that her sister Fredah, who was healthy, travelled to collect her body for burial but became ill, dying while receiving treatment.

    "Kathy Murugu had gone to attend a festival in Migori county on the day of 27th December she was found in her hotel room unresponsive. She was found by a cleaner in the hotel she was staying. She was unresponsive and was pronounced dead upon her body being taken to hospital.

    Then Fredah Murugu had travelled up to Migori to collect the body of her sister and she checked into a hotel in Migori. The following morning she woke up feeling unwell and went to Migori District Hospital, and she passed on while receiving treatment," he narrated.

    The spokesperson appealed for patience as they await a post-mortem report, discouraging people from speculation surrounding their demise.

    Kathy, who was based in Washington DC, had three children, Gwendolyn Murugu, the late Bobby King Murugu and Makena Murugu while Fredah leaves behind four children, Raymond Muriuki, Xenia Wangari, Eli Mutethia and Zoe Njeri.

    Chuka Igambang'ombe MP loses daughter, father

    In another tragic news, Chuka Igambang'ombe MP Patrick Munene lost his dad and daughter in the same week.

    Munene's dad passed away on Tuesday, November 1, 2022, while his daughter Natasha died on Saturday, 5, 2022.

    Munene's father was laid to rest on Friday, November 11, 2022, while Natasha, who died in India, was buried at their rural home on November 18.
