    Kaimati is a unique delicacy well known to be associated with the people from the Kenyan coast. Kaimati recipe Kenya is not a typical Kenyan dish, and those of us that are familiar with the history of our country will tell you that the Arabs and Indians frequented coastal Kenya. And because there was intermingling between the Arabs with the Africans from the coast, the locals got assimilated to their ways and slowly started to embrace and adapt to Indian spices and dishes.

    For those of us who do not know how Kaimati looks, you ought to visit Mombasa in your free time because Kaimati is sold in every street of Mombasa. Most people often use Kaimati recipe Swahili delicacies because they want to replicate the sweetness of the dumplings they tasted in Mombasa.

    However, while Kaimati is a popular snack in the streets of Mombasa, they go by different names in Europe and the Middle East. For instance, Europeans call kalamata Lokma or Loukoumades while in the Middle East, its Lugmat or Luqaimat. If you follow the Muslim calendar, then you may have seen Kaimati because it is mostly cooked by the Muslims in the month of Ramadhan.

    But if you wish to make Kaimati also known as homemade sweet dumpling, don’t wait until Ramadhan. Use this straight forward Kaimati recipe Kenya and make the delicious snacks that you and your loved ones can eat in the morning, evening, and anytime you have sugar cravings. Besides, various Kaimati recipe variations can be adapted to suit certain preferences.

    Whether you are using Kaimati recipe with yoghurt or without, the whole cooking process is divided into two major sections: making sugar syrup and dumplings.

    How to make kaimaiti (Jinsi ya kupika Kalimati)

    In making this Swahili delicacy, you should have the following Kaimati ingredients;

    • 1 cup of home baking flour
    • 2 tablespoons of unflavored yogurt
    • 1 teaspoon of dried instant yeast
    • ½ cup of clean water
    • ½ teaspoon of pumpkin pie spice or cinnamon powder
    • Two tablespoons of sugar
    • One tablespoon lemon juice

    Instructions

  • Mix the flour, dried yeast, and pumpkin pie spice or cinnamon powder in a bowl.
  • Continue mixing with your hands until the batter is sticky.
  • Cover the smooth batter and set it aside for 1 hour for it to rise or cloud up.
  • While waiting for the batter to puff up, prepare the syrup for coating using the recipe listed below.
  • In the meantime, heat the vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot. You will know the oil is hot enough to fry when you insert a wooden spoon and bubbles form around it.
  • Drop the batter into bulbous shape into the hot oil without overcrowding the available space.

    8. Drop the fried batters into the cooled syrup for two to three minutes and remove them.

    Ingredients for Kaimati sugar syrup glaze

    • 1 cup of sugar
    • ½ cup of water
    • ½ teaspoon of cardamom powder.

    Directions

  • Add all the ingredients in a pot or pan and boil.
  • Cook on medium heat for 9 minutes or until looking sticky. Remember, as soon as it starts boiling, avoid stirring as it speeds up the crystallization process.
  • While boiling, scoop a small portion and check whether it has reached the syrup consistency required and to avoid overcooking it, which might turn the syrup into solid. The trick is to remove the syrup once it has a two-thread consistency.

    Kaimati recipe without yogurt

    Most Kaimati recipes often call for coconut milk and yoghurt to give the delicacy softness and outside crunchiness. However, some people sometimes prefer cooking Swahili delicacy Kaimati without using yoghurt.

    Maybe you might be allergic to yoghurt, or it’s not conveniently available. If you ever find yourself in that position, no worries. Some of the substitutes you can put in place of yogurt in your recipe to serve tasteful crispy Kaimati include acidified milk, watered-down sour cream, kefir, or cream of tartar.

    However, you can still get Kaimati recipe without yoghurt that uses cornstarch, sour cream, or even sour milk, which is popularly known as Maziwa Mala in Kenya. So in case you run out of yoghurt, substitute it with these alternatives, and you’ll get the same quality results.

    In this recipe, the only fundamental change is the substitution of yogurt. But the remaining ingredients and procedure for cooking a simple Kaimati remain as mentioned above.

    Kaimati without yeast

    In making such kind of Kaimati, you ought to follow the discussed procedure to the latter by adding all the ingredients according to the recipe except yeast. In its substitute, you can cook your Kaimati with baking powder or baking soda or a mixture of milk and vinegar.

    The trick to a no-fail sweet dumplings recipe is to use that which will give small, tasteful bites that are soft in the inside and crispy brown on the outside. Since they have a sugar coating, they can be too sweet if taken with sweetened tea. The bitterness in coffee often balances out the sugar, but you can still eat Kaimati with milk tea or a glass of milk.

    If you are already an exemplary Kaimati maker, sometimes it’s worth making sugar-coated Kaimati with and without yoghurt to compare the different tastes.
