    2. Access the link from a browser or Instagram app

    Some users have found that they get the error when they access a link from a desktop browser. In that case, consider opening your Instagram account on your mobile app and then click on the link.

    If it opens, then Instagram has an issue with its desktop service. You can also do vice versa if you cannot open the page from your app. In other words, try opening it from your desktop if the app is not working.

    3. Check the post after a week

    Instagram sometimes shadow bans users for a week. The term generally limits a user's content reach by restricting its visibility. In other words, Instagram can ban a profile, page, or picture without informing the person that uploaded it. Then, when you try to access the link or page, you get the error saying, sorry, this page isn't available on Instagram.

    Deactivating an account is not the same as deleting an account. Once deactivated, the information posted by the user remains but is not visible. If they activate it later, you can visit the page or link and find the content you want to view.

    5. Check the link and try again

    You may have entered the wrong URL or link when accessing a page. For example, you may have missed a letter, number, or even a hyphen. Therefore, check the link to ensure you have entered the correct one and try again. Also, you can ask the page owner to repost the link and try again.

    6. Check if the profile has blocked you

    When an Instagram user has blocked you, several things will happen:

    • First, you cannot find the user on Instagram using the search bar.
    • Second, you cannot see whom they are following or their followers.
    • Third, you cannot view their posts.
    • Fourth, you cannot view their Instagram stories.
    • Fifth, you can send a message to them, but they will not receive it.
    • Finally, you cannot see your direct conversations with the person.
