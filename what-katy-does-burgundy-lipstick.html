    It took me 38 years to risk a red lipstick. And reader, just two more to brave a burgundy.

    Last week, during a stint at NYFW, I went over to the dark side. And it wasn’t planned.

    Having packed my small, but perfectly formed, make-up uniform for the week (take one concealer, some foundation, one black liner, a mascara, two risk-averse lipsticks and zero imagination), I thought I had my beauty game planned. It was either going to be a fresh red lipstick and denim kinda day, or a nude lip and liner on black cashmere.

    By day three, after some experimenting at the Nars boutique on Prince Street, a little cosmetic courage and a lot more persuasion from my friend and fashionable partner in beauty crime, Sophie Beresiner, a fellow beauty director (of Elle UK), my lips had made alternative arrangements. Its name was Train Bleu, it was sexy, it was wild, and its deep burgundy tone was reminiscent of the 1920s femme fatale. Just the thing to go with my Serge Normant flapper-girl blow-dry (a must for any beauty editor visiting the Big Apple) apparently.

    "Fresh skin, just a slick of glossy mascara, forget blush and just own it. Aubergine lipstick just looks amazing on blondes," she told me. "Amazing?" From the girl who looked good in every lip colour she ever wore, it was all the persuasion I needed.

    But I still needed practise, some inner confidence if you like, and so I applied the chubby pencil a little later that day, when alone in my hotel room. Reader, this is a genius and most effective tip, once extolled to me by the other Lipstick Queen in my life, Poppy King, founder of her namesake make-up range. "The only reason women think they don’t suit a bold lip colour is that they simply aren’t used to it. So, practise practise, practise by wearing it at home [insert: hotel room], and catch yourself in the mirror. You’ll learn to love it." Employing those same successful tactics that got me onto red all those beauty chapters ago, a few reflective hours later that evening, I quite fancied the idea of Train Bleu.

    By morning, against clean skin, jet-black mascara and a chunky knit (again, thanks Mz B) I was ‘owning’ Nars' Velvet Matte Lip Pencil Train Bleu, while, by the way, also winning a couple of compliments.

    It just goes to show, sometimes it’s about coming out of your shell to get into the darkness. Be plucky – your new palette is waiting.

    Nars
