    Fight like a girl! Two female high school wrestlers in Arizona and Maine beat boys to win state titl

    Two high school senior girls defeated boys this week to win state wrestling championships in Maine and Arizona. In Maine, 17-year-old Maddie Ripley ofOceanside High School defeated three boys in one day to defend the state title she won last year.

    03 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Two high school senior girls defeated boys this week to win state wrestling championships in Maine and Arizona.

    In Maine, 17-year-old Maddie Ripley of Oceanside High School defeated three boys in one day to defend the state title she won last year.

    'I didn't really hear too much that it was a fluke,' she told ABC News. 'Now that I've won a second, then it just proves that it wasn't.'


    According to the network, Ripley competed in the 113-pound weight class of the Class B high school wrestling championship tournament in Rumford, Maine.

    She is the first girl in Maine to win a high school wrestling title against boys.

    Maddie Ripley of Oceanside High School in Maine defeated three boys in one day

    And Audrey Jimenez became the first girl to win a state wrestling championship in Arizona

    Jimenez, who won in the 107-pound division, is currently training for the 2024 Olympic trials

    In fact, Ripley won the first-ever title in the girls' division earlier in the same week before putting on some weight to compete with the boys.

    She told ABC that she gained six pounds after lifting weights.

    Incredibly, Ripley's title in the boys' division occurred on the same day that Audrey Jimenez pulled off the same feat in Arizona.

    Jimenez, a senior at Sunnyside High School in Tucson, Arizona, beat boys in the 107-pound weight class to win a title at the AIA Arizona High School State Wrestling Championships.

    She became the first girl to do so.

    Ripley competed in the 113-pound weight class of the Class B high school tournament

    Jimenez was previously only allowed to compete against other girls in the postseason

    'The crowd was just super crazy,' Jimenez told Scripps News Phoenix. 'So that's what made it seem more of like 'Oh wow, like I just won state.''

    Curiously, Jimenez had been allowed to face boys during the regular season but not the postseason for the last three years.

    However, coaches successfully petitioned the Arizona Interscholastic Association this year to let Jimenez compete in the boys' championship.

    'Once you see it done,' she said, 'you know it's possible.'

    Jimenez is currently training for the 2024 Olympic trials, according to ABC News, while Ripley hopes to one day be an Olympian as well.
