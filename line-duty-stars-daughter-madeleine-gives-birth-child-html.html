    Madeleine told how she was down on one knee on a jetty on the Thames outside London's Oxo Tower – where the couple had their first kiss – in June when security guards called the police, saying they were trespassing as the jetty was closed.

    Madeleine, who met her fiance in June last year, said her mother, Dutch-Australian actress Anna Nygh, 73, wondered at first why she didn't wait for Callum to pop the question.

    But she said: 'I think I preferred the special feeling of asking myself.' 

    Madeleine has followed in her parents' footsteps having starred as Daniella in 2011 drama A Landscape of Lies and as Olivia in Ghosts. 

    She previously had a number of small roles including in Cemetery Junction, Doctors and in shorts Vicki's Disease and Match.

    Madeleine has also recently been cast as Gwendolen in an upcoming production of Oscar Wilde's The Importance of Being Earnest. 

    Her father Adrian moved to London to pursue a career in acting, and is became a household name as Superintendent Ted Hastings in Line Of Duty.

    Adrian tied the knot with his Australian wife Anna, who is an actress and casting director, in 1986 and the pair have been married for 35 years. 

    Adrian is also a stepfather to Anna's son Ted, whom she shares with a partner from a previous relationship.
