    Husband and girlfriend convicted of murdering his wife whose remains found at storage facility

    An estranged husband and his girlfriend have been convicted of murdering his teacher wife and hiding her charred remains surrounded by air fresheners at a Staten Island storage facility, prosecutors announced Monday.

    12 Mar 2024, 00:00

    An estranged husband and his girlfriend have been convicted of murdering his teacher wife and hiding her charred remains surrounded by air fresheners at a Staten Island storage facility, prosecutors announced Monday.

    Michael Cammarata, 44, and his girlfriend Ayisha Egea, 43, were charged with second-degree murder, first-degree manslaughter, and concealment of a human corpse, two counts of tampering with physical evidence, and five counts of endangering the welfare of a child, according to NBC. 

    Cammarata was also charged with fourth- and fifth-degree criminal possession of stolen property, and first-, third- and fourth- degree stalking.

    The pair have pleaded not guilty to the charges but Cammarata was found guilty before a jury in New York Supreme Court on all charges, NBC reported.

    Egea will be tried separately at a later date. 

    Michael Cammarata (left), 44, and his girlfriend Ayisha Egea (right), 43, have been convicted of murdering his estranged wife and hiding her charred remains surrounded by air fresheners at a Staten Island storage facility, prosecutors announced Monday

    Cammarata and Egea murdered 37-year-old mother-of-three Jeanine Cammarata (pictured) 48 hours after she served Cammarata divorce papers. Her 'charred and unrecognizable' body was found five days after she was reported missing

    Jeanine's body was found in a storage facility in Staten Island, New York (pictured). The van that Cammarata allegedly drove from Queens to Staten Island on Saturday night had been recovered by police but Jeanine's car was not immediately found

    Police at Extra Space Storage on Staten Island where the body was found 

    They both face a maximum penalty of life in prison and a minimum sentence of just 25 years for what Richmond County District Attorney Michael E McMahon called a 'heinous crime' with a 'calculated and cold-hearted plot'.

    In March of 2019 Cammarata and Egea killed 37-year-old mother-of-three Jeanine Cammarata in an act of domestic violence.

    Forty-eight hours earlier Jeanine had served Cammarata divorce papers. Although the couple had two children, at the time she and her estranged husband had already seemed to be living separate lives. 

    Cammarata was dating Egea, who was six months pregnant when she helped in the murder; and Jeanine had a boyfriend, who remained anonymous.

    Jeanine's boyfriend was the last person to see her alive after she dropped him off at his apartment on McVeigh Avenue in Heartland Village around 9pm on Saturday, March 30, 2019.

    He reported her missing to police three days later after she failed to show up for work at elementary school PS 29 in Castleton Corner - or her side job at the Dollar Tree.

    She had also missed a child custody hearing on Monday, according to Eric Gansberg, the attorney handling her divorce. 

    'It just doesn't make sense to me,' Gansberg told the New York Post.

    'She was so eager to proceed. She wanted to be with her children. I cannot imagine her just walking away,' he added.

    According to court documents, Cammarata (right with Jeanine) admitted to cops that he had hit her in the face after they had sex less than a week before she vanished

    Jeanine was last seen alive around 9pm on Saturday, March 30, 2019 - five days before her body was found - after she dropped her anonymous boyfriend off at his apartment on McVeigh Avenue in Heartland Village

    By the afternoon of Tuesday, April 2, Cammarata was questioned by police about Jeanine's disappearance. He told cops he had hit her in the face after they had sex on Saturday.

    In the days Jeanine was missing her best friend Jessica Pobega told cops she received strange and mysterious text messages from Jeanine on the day she vanished.  

    When Pobega didn't hear from her friend and said she was going to call the police out of concern, the mother allegedly replied: 'I don't want that. I have the kids...I apologize. I have to do this for the children.'

    Pobega appeared to be suspicious if it was actually Jeanine who sent her the messages because she never ended up seeing her children over the weekend.

    'I don't know who that was and what they were covering for,' she said, adding: 'She's my oldest friend. My mother is distraught. Something happened. Something bad happened. And I don't know what it is and I really hope I'm wrong.'

    On Thursday, April 4, Jeanine's 'charred and unrecognizable' body was discovered at a storage facility in Staten Island surrounded by air fresheners, according to court papers. 

    The documents revealed how Cammarata changed his story when probed by cops over her death.  

    Cammarata (pictured after his arrest in April 2019) changed his story when probed by cops over his estranged wife's death. He first told police he last saw Jeanine at a supermarket near his apartment but later admitted he lied because he didn't want his girlfriend to find out he was still having sex with Jeanine

    He first told police he last saw his Jeanine on March 30 in a supermarket near his apartment. The following day he claimed to have driven to Staten Island for a dance recital before returning home to Queens that night, the Voluntary Disclosure Form shows.

    But license-plate readers contradicted his statements and the form alleged Cammarata then admitted he lied because he did not want Egea to find out he was still having sex with Jeanine.

    When Cammarata's new story didn't match what license-plate readers showed for the second time, he changed his story yet again.  

    Cammarata then claimed a row broke out at and he pushed Jeanine and hit her several times, court documents said.

    However, Egea reportedly told a different version of events to police. She said the three of them drove around the block in Jeanine's car before she left to go home with Cammarata. 

    But prosecutors said Jeanine was killed after going to visit her children, who were living with Cammarata in Queens.

    Investigators obtained surveillance footage which showed Cammarata carrying a large plastic bin out of his apartment.

    The van that Cammarata allegedly drove from Queens to Staten Island on Saturday night had been recovered by police but Jeanine's car was not immediately found. 

    The defendants reportedly took Jeanine's debit card and driver’s license before dropping her body off at the storage facility.

    Cammarata was later seen unloading the bin with the help of another person at a storage facility in Staten Island, where police recovered Jeanine's body later that week. 

    Jeanine was reported missing after she missed a child custody hearing on Monday, according to Eric Gansberg, the attorney handling her divorce. Her and Cammarata had two children together (pictured above at the couple's wedding). Jeanine also had an 11-year-old son from another marriage

    McMahon said in a statement: 'Today’s guilty verdict ensures that his heinous crime will not go unpunished, and that the defendant will be held fully accountable for not only killing his wife, but the calculated and cold-hearted plot to cover up the crime.'

    Those who know Jeanine say she was terrified of her husband. They say she described his behavior toward her as abusive.

    Her landlord, Jose Perez, claimed that Jeanine was 'concerned about her safety' because Michael would harass her and follow her in his car. 'She always texted me where she's at or if something's happening to her. But she has not,' Perez said.

    The landlord said he last saw Jeanine two weeks before her disappearance and that she was determined to win custody of her two children with Michael.   

    'He wasn't giving her her kids, she wasn't able to see her kids,' Perez said.

    'That's when she said: "Okay, I had enough. I'm gonna go get a lawyer, I'm gonna get divorced, and I'm gonna get custody of my kids." And now she's gone,' he added.

    Jeanine also had an 11-year-old son from another marriage.
