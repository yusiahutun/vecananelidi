    Triple stabbing near Watford's football ground as men rushed to hospital in early morning

    Armed police and a dog unit were rushed to Vicarage Road in the Hertfordshire town, north-west of London, at around a 3.15am this morning. Part of Vicarage Road, close to the Premier League stadium, was still cordoned off this morning.

    04 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Armed police and a dog unit were rushed to Vicarage Road in the Hertfordshire town, north-west of London, at around a 3.15am this morning.

    Part of Vicarage Road, close to the Premier League stadium, was still cordoned off this morning.

    The road was shut between Farraline Way and Occupational Road.

    Long traffic queues have built up in the area.

    Patients due to attend nearby Watford General Hospital were advised to use a different entrance on Thomas Sawyer Way.

    Herts police said in a statement: "At 3.17am today (July 3) police were called to Vicarage Road, Watford following a report of an incident where several people had sustained stab wounds.

    "Officers attended alongside the ambulance service.

    "Three people were taken to hospital.

    "Armed officers and a dog unit were sent to the scene to support local officers due to the nature of the incident.

    "The road is currently closed and members of the public are asked to avoid the area as police continue their investigation.

    "Witnesses to the incident, or anyone else with information, are asked to contact Hertfordshire Constabulary via 101, quoting reference ISR 49 of July 3."
