    Karen Swensen- Relationship, Married Life

    Karen was married to her late husband, John Ronquillo, who died of cancer in 2018. Her husband worked as a cop in New Orleans. Karen currently lives with her daughter Catherine and son Matthew after being married for nearly 18 years.

    Karen Swensen- Professional Career

    Karen began her media career as a morning news anchor for the NECN station in Boston. She also worked as a documentary producer for the network, where she spent five years. Meanwhile, she directed the documentary “Katrina Flood of Tears.” In the 1990s, she began working as a desk assistant at WWL-TV, a CBS affiliate. She read the nightly, one-minute hourly news while working for the channel. Karen was eventually promoted to Eyewitness News as a reporter and began co-anchoring Eyewitness Investigation News with a friend and colleague, Hoda Kotb.

    During her time at WWL, she aggressively covered the Katrina hurricane. She has received a great deal of respect and acclaim for her work as an investigative reporter throughout the years. Swensen has received the Gabriel Award, the Garcia Award, the Associated Press Award, and the Club Award in recognition of her work. She has also received five Emmy Awards. She owns a T-shirt company called Life’s About Change in addition to her journalism work. Her company’s profits are donated to cancer research institutes. This month, she announced her retirement from journalism, and she has received a lot of affection from her fans, admirers, and colleagues.
