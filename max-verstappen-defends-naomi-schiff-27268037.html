    Max Verstappen has spoken out in support of Sky Sports F1 presenter Naomi Schiff after she was abused online.

    Schiff, who has joined Sky's broadcast team this year, was last week the victim of a social media troll who questioned her credentials and why she'd been given the role. Seven-time world champion Lewis Hamilton leapt to her defence on Twitter.

    And reigning champ Verstappen has now addressed the issue too ahead of the Canadian Grand Prix this weekend. He said: "Most of the time, the people who are hating on you are frustrated people in life, or work, or whatever.

    "Of course it's bad. I didn't even read the comments but I picked up a bit along the lines. It's not nice but we all receive hate. I receive hate, but do I really care? No, because these people, they don't even deserve the attention to be honest.

    Schiff, 28, is more than qualified to talk all things F1 as a race driver herself, having competed in the 2019 W Series. Another former world champion in four-time winner Sebastian Vettel has also slammed her critics.

    She'll be back on presenting duties in Montreal this weekend, where Verstappen will be looking to extend his championship lead with a second straight victory after winning last time out in Baku.
