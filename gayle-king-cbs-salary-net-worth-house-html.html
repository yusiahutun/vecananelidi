    With over three decades of experience in the journalism industry, American television personality, Gayle King possesses an ever blooming career that has rewarded her with prosperity every way around.

    The Maryland native currently co-anchors the morning show on CBS News, CBS This Morning.

    Gayle King's Professional Life Took Her To WDAF-TV, CBS, NBC  

    Gayle initialized her journalism career working as a reporter and weekend anchor at WDAF-TV. She also worked at WJZ-TV in Baltimore, Md., and WTOP-TV in Washington, D.C.

    Later in 1981, she was hired as a television news anchor for WFSB-TV in Hartford, Connecticut which is a CBS affiliate. She contributed 18 years of her life there while also hosting her syndicated daytime program.

    Gayle also co-hosted NBC's daytime talk show called Cover to Cover, with Robin Wagner in 1991 but unfortunately, the show got canceled after 13 weeks. Later in 1997, she was offered her talk show, The Gayle King Show, which aired only for a season.

    Gayle King Short Bio (Age) & Her Married Life

    The veteran television host was born on 28 December 1954 as a daughter of father, Scott King and mother Peggy King. She grew up under the wings of her parents in Turkey where she joined American school and later returned to Maryland. She is a graduate of the University of Maryland where she earned a double degree in sociology and psychology in 1976. 

    Despite her successful career, Gayle has not enjoyed prosperity in her marital life. She was married to an American attorney, William Bumpus in the year 1982. Their relationship lasted for 11 years as they got divorced in 1993.

    Gayle and William shares two children, a son named William Bumpus Jr. and a daughter named Kirby Bumpus.
