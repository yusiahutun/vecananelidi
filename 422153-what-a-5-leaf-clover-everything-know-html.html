    The wonders of creation continue to amaze us every day. Peculiar plants like the 5 leaf clover are rare and attract numerous superstitions and beliefs about what they represent. Netizens and nature lovers have been seeking to find out, ''What does a 5 leaf clover mean?''

    Clovers, also known as trefoils, are herbaceous plants that fall in the pea family species, Fabaceae, originally from Europe. They exist in over 300 species and grow naturally in most temperate and subtropical regions. Clovers are small in size, and usually, they have three leaflets. However, every so often, you may come across one with more leaves. So, what does a 5 leaf clover mean?

    About the clover

    Clovers mean different things in different cultures. For example, the Greeks used it in garlands and other decorations, while the middle age generation believed that a person carrying the four-leaf clover could see fairies.

    In 1620, Sir John Melton made the first reference to their ability to provide good fortune. Around that time, an English writer wrote, “If any man walking in the fields, find any four-leaved grass, he shall in a small while after find some good thing.”

    Clovers are high in protein, phosphorus and calcium. Therefore, in their green stage, the compound plants are ideal for livestock feeds. In their dry stage, farmers can also use them as hay, pasture or silage.

    The plants also make an excellent cover crop. In addition, clover flowers are highly attractive to bees, and clover honey is a commonly-used secondary product of clover cultivation.

    They grow as small, fragrant flowers and appear in four colours; white, pink, red and yellow. The white and red variations are the most common. A young three-leaved clover was initially called a shamrock.

    The 5 leaf clover

    Clovers mutate genetically into numerous variations. Apart from the common three-leafed plant, there are also four and five-leaved mutations. So far, the highest number of discovered clover leaflets is 56, and it appears in the Guinness World Records. Shigeo Obara from Japan found the plant in 2009.

    Each of the leaves has various meanings attached to it. For example, some people believe that the three-leaved clover represents faith, hope and love. The fourth leaflet on the other variant reportedly stands for good luck.

    How rare are 5 leaf clovers?

    The five-leaf clover is the rarest of these variants. Surprisingly, the odds of finding a five-leaf clover are one in a million for new finders. However, others believe that collectors have a one in 20,000 chance of finding the treasure.

    What does a five-leaf clover mean?

    The five-leaf clover has sometimes been referred to as green gold or rose clover. Some sources believe that the leaves on the rare herb represent faith, hope, love, luck and wealth. For those with six leaves, the sixth one reportedly means fame.

    Is a five-leaf clover lucky?

    Yes. According to Irish tradition, individuals who discover this rare species are likely to make a lot of money and become wealthy. Thus, the 5 leaf clover superstition is that it is symbolic of extra luck and financial gain.

    The number five means many things to different people. For some, it represents harmony, balance and peace, which could represent a financial establishment. But, according to Avia, an astrologer from What's Your Sign, the five-leaved clover symbolizes transition, adventure, travel, and unpredictability.

    According to the website, a 5-leaf clover is symbolic of focus and union with all directions. The element of the plant is that it stands for knowing, intuition and precognition. The herb also represents a season where individuals are in the present moment and surrendering to each transition.

    Most finders keep and preserve the scarce plant and wait for their big financial break. However, financial experts advise that the collectors take part in income-generating investments or career ventures and not just sit and wait for the breakthrough.

    Among the numerous beliefs surrounding the rare compound plant, none of them is symbolic of bad luck. Collectors and botanists who study the clover all attach positive effects and results to the herb.
