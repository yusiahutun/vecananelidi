    How does a thickening shampoo work?

    Hair loses nutrients from a number of factors, from excessive washing to sun exposure, so thickening shampoos work to make up the lack of nutrients, says Rubenstein. "Protein, specifically keratin, is something fine hair desperately needs to look fuller," she adds. "It also prevents breakage, which is much more common in fine and thin hair."

    Do you need conditioner for flat, limp hair?

    "Thickening shampoos are wonderful, but they don't work best on their own," Rubenstein explains. "Many people with fine or thin hair think they donâ€™t need conditioner, but this is an absolute myth. Without conditioner hair is left vulnerable to more damage and will cause hair to become more broken, weaker, and finer." The trick to finding a great conditioner to work alongside a thickening shampoo is to look for a formula with limited oils. Overtime, shampoo can be drying to hair, so supplement conditioner with a hair mask if your strands feel extra dry, says Novick.
