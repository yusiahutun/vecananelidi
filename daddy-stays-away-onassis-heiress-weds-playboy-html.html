    Daddy stays away as Onassis heiress weds her playboy

    Shipping Heiress Athina Roussel-Onassis has been snubbed by her once devoted father on her wedding day. The 20-year-old billionairess, last of the fabulously wealthy Onassis dynasty, walked down the aisle on the arm of her fianc's father instead.

    02 May 2024, 00:00

    Shipping Heiress Athina Roussel-Onassis has been snubbed by her once devoted father on her wedding day.

    The 20-year-old billionairess, last of the fabulously wealthy Onassis dynasty, walked down the aisle on the arm of her fiancé's father instead.

    Some of the 750 guests gasped, others stared in disbelief over the absence of French-born Thierry Roussel.

    Speculation about whether he would attend the extravagant nuptials in Sao Paulo in Brazil had been growing after he refused to add his name to the invitations.

    Yesterday it emerged that while his daughter was having the most important day of her life he was 4,500 miles away on holiday in Casablanca with his wife Gaby.

    His absence underlined just how far the family has been driven apart by Athina's choice of husband - 32-year-old Brazilian playboy Alvaro Neto, known as Doda.

    The showjumping champion, who has a six-year-old daughter from a relationship with model Sibele Dorsa, met Athina at an equestrian centre in Belgium two years ago.

    She fell for him almost immediately and they now live in a £3million duplex in Sao Paulo's wealthiest areas.

    'Gold-digger'

    Some suspect that in the eyes of Mr Roussel, 52, and the Greek trustees of the Onassis fortune, the showjumper is a gold-digger who has bewitched a vulnerable young woman. A source close to Mr Roussel said: "He could not bear to see her marrying that man."

    Only one of Mr Roussel's children with Gaby - 18-year-old Sandrine - attended the wedding.

    Athina's other half-siblings Erik, 20, and Johanna, 14, were believed to have been in Morocco with their parents. The source added: "Many were invited but felt they couldn't go if Thierry wasn't going to be there."

    Athina, dressed in a strapless Valentino gown, was said to have been given away on Saturday by Doda's father Ricardo Miranda.

    There were two marquees, one for the ceremony and one for the reception, in the grounds of the Maria Luiza and Oscar Americano Foundation, an 18-acre estate.

    The 'church' was decorated with an abundance of white flowers and there was an enormous white cross at the altar. The couple exchanged vows in an ecumenical service because she belongs to the Greek Orthodox church and he is a Roman Catholic.

    Doda was heard whispering "You are everything to me," to his bride. "What impressed me was their unity and the love between them," one guest said. Brazil's Veja magazine reported that the couple signed a prenuptial agreement.

    Athina's biographer Alexis Mantheakis said the wedding was a 'very un-Onassis affair'. Bitter at not being invited, he said: "The Greeks feel snubbed and so too does her family. It speaks volumes about the couple's relationship that her father did not go."

    Athina has long been known as the 'poor little rich girl'. Her mother Christina died at 37 in 1988, when Athina was three, after a history of drug abuse. Her wealth came from her father, Aristotle.

    During Mr Roussel's three-year marriage to Christina, he refused to give up his mistresses, fathering three children by Gaby Landhage, to whom he is now married.

    They brought Athina up with their children in Switzerland. When she was 18 she inherited the bulk of her fortune.
