    Moments after Taylor Swift announced her newest album, The Tortured Poets Department, fans found evidence connecting the title to none other than her most recent ex, Joe Alwyn.

    In a Variety interview between Alwyn and Paul Mescal, published in December 2022, the actors talk about a group chat they’re in with Fleabag and All of Us Strangers star Andrew Scott, endearingly dubbed “The Tortured Man Club.”

    “So what’s the name of the WhatsApp group we’re in?” Mescal asks.

    Alwyn replies, “It’s either the ‘tortured’ or the ‘lonely’—‘Tortured Man Club,’ I think. Me, you, and Andrew. It hasn’t had much use recently.”

    “No,” Mescal affirms. “I feel like we're less tortured now.”

    (Notably, Swift is also close friends with Mescal’s recent ex, Boygenius member Phoebe Bridgers.)

    Swifties quickly descended on the resurfaced video, formulating theories about the impending songs’ relationship to Alwyn. “joe alwyn are you ready for your tape,” one fan wrote.

    This isn’t the first time Alwyn has figured as a muse for Swift. The two dated for six years—before calling off their relationship last spring—during which time the Conversations With Friends star frequently played a central role in many of the singer’s love songs, across her albums Reputation, Lover, Folklore, and Evermore. Most recently, fans speculated that Swift’s 2023 single “You’re Losing Me” was inspired by her split from Alwyn, as the song was released shortly after news of the breakup emerged.

    Although Swift and Alwyn dated for over half a decade, they were extremely private about their relationship—a trait that Swift, who is now dating NFL player Travis Kelce, seemed to take a dig at in an interview with Time last year. “When you say a relationship is public, that means I’m going to see him do what he loves, we’re showing up for each other, other people are there and we don’t care,” she said. “The opposite of that is, you have to go to an extreme amount of effort to make sure no one knows that you’re seeing someone. And we’re just proud of each other.”

    In addition to announcing the April 19 release of Poets, her 11th studio album, at Sunday night’s 66th Grammy Awards show, Swift also made history there as the first artist to win Album of the Year four times, this time for 2022’s Midnights. She ultimately went home with two new golden gramophones under her belt, as Midnights also landed the award for Best Pop Vocal Album.
