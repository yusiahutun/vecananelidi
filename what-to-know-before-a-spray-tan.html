    I am a 28-year-old beauty editor and until last week I had never had a spray tan. Worse, I'd never even used fake tan. I had only ever tiptoed cautiously into the safe and shallow waters of a gradual tanner once or twice before, panicking at the first sign of a streak and scampering away as fast as my pasty little legs could carry me.

    I am the kind of pale that can occasionally cause people to enquire if I'm feeling unwell. Under the actual sun (or for me, mostly the shade, which I rarely venture out of), I can achieve a freckly face and brown shoulders through SPF30, both of which promptly disappear as soon as I'm past customs at Heathrow. Some people are bronzed, and I am not. I am, largely, OK with it. But last week, for a glorious three to five days, I knew how it felt to be tanned following a spritzing of Sienna X courtesy of its wonderful brand ambassador Carly Hobbs. Here are a few things I wish I'd known before…

    Wear a top you don't mind being braless in

    I had somehow missed the memo (and lacked the common sense) telling me that my journey home would be best travelled braless. Thankfully, I was wearing a dark blouse, which was fairly floaty and frilly – and looked just great paired with my loose Puma tracksuit bottoms, by the way – but anything clingier and I might have felt the need to clutch my chest on the Piccadilly Line home.

    Don't take too many bags with you

    A tote bag, a Tesco bag and a cross-body bag left me paranoid that I would have patchy lines on the crease of my elbow and right across my middle when I undressed. Thankfully, I was fine – but I suspect this was largely due to the expertise of Hobbs' application rather than anything else. I may not be so lucky next time.

    The sight of it rinsing down the plughole will depress you

    The evening of, I felt an alarming shade of bronze and had visions of waking up, like Ross from Friends, screeching, "I'm an eight?!" In reality, the colour darkened to no more beyond a pleasing shade of tan (never before seen on my body) and the gravy-brown water I was expecting didn't really materialise. The little that did escape during my shower just took the edge off, but still made me feel as though my days of golden glow were vanishing far too rapidly.

    Moisturise, even if you hate doing so

    This I did know, but I didn't fully understand just how important it was. It's my least favourite beauty task, but needs must and my tanning-pro sister demanded that I do it for the greater good. Thankfully, the Sanctuary's Wet Skin Moisture Miracle had landed on my desk mere days before. The in-shower formula, applied when wet, sank in immediately and prevented me from becoming in any way marbled.

    Sticky feet are a marvel

    I was unaware of the existence of such things, but the genius little self-adhesive footpads that prevent bright orange feet after a tan boggled my mind! I want some for the gym showers, the swimming-pool showers, taking the bins out…

    Choose your tanner well, and your first won't be your last

    A week after my first-ever spray tan, I only feel annoyed with myself that I left it so long. I can't picture becoming an addict, but I do feel that my (many hundreds of) days of blancmange legs when arriving on holiday are in the past. And good riddance.
