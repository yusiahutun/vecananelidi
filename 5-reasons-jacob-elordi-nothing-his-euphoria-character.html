    We all know it’s all too easy to hate Euphoria’s leading misogynist, Nate Jacobs. So we’re understandably confused whenever we reminded just how nice Jacob Elordi, the heartthrob playing him, is in real life. With his consistently grounded and philosophical outlook on life, this rising star seems like a much-needed breath of fresh air in Hollywood.

    Here are five no-nonsense ways Elordi proves that an actor is not his character.

    1. He’s not trying to be hyper masculine

    Elordi might soon join the ranks of Harry Styles, Ezra Miller and other forward-thinking celebrities with his fashion philosophy. The actor is not afraid to push the boundaries of masculinity with his 70s-inspired everyday street fashion and styling on magazine covers.

    Check out this recent gender-fluid appearance in Man About Town magazine. We’re loving the hair flick, too.

    Playboy Playmate to farm girl: meet the new Crystal Hefner!

    2. He respects women

    Unlike his infamous Euphoria alter ego, Elordi considers women his equals.

    Despite going to an all-boys school in Melbourne, the actor recently told Men’s Health that he could never quite understand the way his classmates would talk about women.

    “I have a brother and a dad as well, but I grew up in a house of women, you know?” he says, referring to his mother and three sisters. “Seeing the way they spoke about things at the school, I was like, this doesn’t make any sense. How could you speak like that?”

    Ever the gentleman, when asked about the rumour his ex-girlfriend Kaia Gerber was seeing someone new, during the same interview, his response was simply: “She handles herself wonderfully publicly. And I’ve learned so much from her about how to handle it, how to deal with it and just kind of be whatever about it, you know?”

    3. He dislikes stars being objectified for their bodies

    Unlike his cold-hearted Euphoria character, whose toxic masculinity is inextricably linked with his intimidating physical presence, the actor sees himself in a very different way. Coming from a family where fitness always came first because his mother was a personal trainer, he has actively voiced his concerns about being defined by his appearance.

    7 celebrities with siblings just as successful and stunning as they are

    All the attention on his body since finding fame is something he worries about as much as the female celebrities he stars alongside. “It’s a slippery slope to put all your value into the vanity of what your body looks like,” he told Men’s Health. “Your body is going to deteriorate.”

    4. He has a sunny disposition

    Elordi seems to always be smiling in real life, whether in candid paparazzi shots or on the red carpet – a stark contrast to Nate Jacobs. Perhaps it’s not surprising given Jacobs’ troubled upbringing versus his actor’s real life one.

    He reportedly talks to his mother often, and has shared photos of himself video calling his parents on Instagram. During a recent appearance on Jimmy Fallon’s The Tonight Show, the shy actor reminisced about his father surprising him with tickets to a New York Knicks game when he was eight, before picking his parents out of the audience to let them know he was taking them this time, and that they’d have the best seats in the house.

    5. He is actually Australian

    6 Hollywood stars settling down with their much younger girlfriends

    Most Euphoria fans have been shocked to discover softly-spoken Elordi’s Brisbane accent in real life. Testament to his acting abilities, we’d say.

    The actor has also said in interviews that he often misses his country of birth. On his current life in Los Angeles, he told Men’s Health, “I know a few people [in LA]. I have a few friends. But I’m not mad about it. I’m here to work, and then I’ll get home when I can.”
