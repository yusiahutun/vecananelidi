    11 Feb 2024, 00:00

    When it comes to our purse strings, there is a lot of value in scouring the British high street for a bargain. While many of us covet designer brands we see on our favourite A-listers, it is a relief to know that we can still look incredible in pieces that our favourite stores such as Arket and River Island.

    Shopping on the high street does not mean we should have to scrimp on style, in fact there are plenty of retailers who are creating eco-conscious and luxurious garments which wouldn't look out of place in a high-end boutique. 

    You may also like

    To make your budget friendly shopping a little easier we've put together a list of our favourite pieces this week.

    How we chose the pieces:

    Aesthetic: After combing through the internet's top high street fashion websites, we have selected the pieces that look categorically luxe. Despite boasting a modest price point, these buys could be mistaken for designer. That could mean a beautiful, flattering bias cut, a carefully constructed print, rich embellishment or another thoughtful design detail.

    Price: We have tried to keep pieces in this edit affordable, under the £250 mark, except for items made from precious materials.

    Why you should trust me:

    I am Hello! Fashion’s Fashion Features Writer. With a love for all things fashion (the quirkier the better), I can usually be found playing dress-up in my own wardrobe, watching Gilmore Girls for the 8th time or finding new outfit inspo and DIY musts on TikTok, nothing in my closet is safe from a safety-pin bow embellishment. I have been in the styling world for almost 5 years now, specialising in editorial features, luxury campaigns and music videos. As for the world of magazines, I have worked for various digital and print publications around the world, focusing on fashion. Basically, fashion is my bread and butter.

    Hello! Fashion shares the most expensive-looking pieces to shop on the high street this week: 

  • Silk Slip Dress

    ARKET

    Why it looks expensive: Because this slip dress is made from 100% silk and cut on the bias, the texture, fit and fabric all exude luxury style.

    Editor's note:Eat your heart out Victoria Beckham, this blush pink slip dress is coming for your crown. I am obsessed with both the cut and colour of this simple, yet elevated silk gown. There’s something super flattering about the scoop neckline paired with the thick straps and bias-cut fabric. I would wear this under a cosy cream knit sweater with heeled boots and a pair of twinkling earrings.

  • Oversized Double-Breasted Leather Coat

    COS

    Why it looks expensive: Chocolate brown will forever and always be trending, especially when in leather. I also think that the double-breasted style is forever iconic in the fashion sphere.

    Editor's note: If you’re still on the hunt for a coat that will last a lifetime in both durability and style, then this leather number is it. Made from 100% leather, this coffee brown coat will go with everything and anything and only get better with age.

  • Zip-top jumper

    H&M

    Why it looks expensive: When styled right, like over a collared blouse this half-zip jumper resembles big old Celine vibes.

    Editor's note: You’ll want to jump on this new arrival quickly because I guarantee it won’t be around for long. Pair with your favourite jeans or a black mini skirt, tights and boots for the ultimate cosy girl 'fit.

  • Cropped t-shirt

    Monki

    Why it looks expensive: Because bows are everywhere right now, both in the luxury fashion world and on the high street, meaning it could be from either end of the fashion spectrum

    Editor's note: It’s been a while since I’ve stumbled across an affordable find that I instantly add to my cart, so when I saw this cropped baby tee adorned with a satin blue bow print I instantly copped. Perfect for when you don’t know what to wear, this simple t-shirt will become your go-to for all occasions.

  • ASOS DESIGN knitted tank top with tipped edges in cable stitch

    ASOS

    Why it looks expensive: The red and blue accents make this vest look like a vintage Tommy Hilfiger find in all the right ways. 

    Editor's note: There’s something about this oversized knit vest that boasts big Harry Styles energy and I am in love. Perfect for those colder days when layering is essential this would look so cute over a white collared shirt or red slip dress.

  • Knitted turtleneck dress

    Mango

    Why it looks expensive: Because of the block colourway and formfitting silhouette, this dress could easily be mistaken for a luxury brand.

    Editor's note: Finding the perfect winter dress is a feat on its own, but this knitted turtleneck number from Mango ticks all the right boxes. I love how the silhouette hugs in all the right places, perfect for wearing on its own, or as a layer under a vest or floor-length slip dress.

  • Cable Knit Cardigan

    Stradivarius

    Why it looks expensive: 2023 might be over and out but the colour red is still very on trend.

    Editor's note: If you’re looking for a wardrobe essential that goes with everything and also combats the seasonal chill, then a cute cable knit cardigan is exactly what you need. I love this red option from Stradivarius because it's a cosy way to add a pop of colour to an outfit without having to compromise on warmth. Wear with the buttons done all the way up or undone over a classic white tank.

  • Out From Under Stretch Lace Bodysuit

    Urban Outfitters

    Why it looks expensive: I think wearing any form of visible lingerie makes an outfit look expensive as it is a conscious styling choice, designed to be seen.

    Editor's note: If Emily Ratajkowski's most recent outfit is anything to go by then we can confidently confirm that lace bodysuits are very in right now. Wear under a low-cut blouse or on its own with a pair of trousers and a matching blazer for a chic date night ensemble.

  • Satin Bucket Bag

    Zara

    Why it looks expensive: When it comes to interesting bag shapes, it's usually the luxe brands leaning in. Personally, anything in a satin fabric and red wine colourway exudes expensive elegance.

    Editor's note: 

    Why it looks expensive:

    When the weather doesn’t allow you to sport a great fit, let your accessories do all the talking. I am in love with the shape of this Zara bucket-style bag and even more in love with the red wine colour. Big enough to house all of your everyday essentials, but chic enough to wear out to a celebratory soiree, this affordable arm candy is exactly what the fashion doctor ordered. 

  • Chunky Drop Chain Earrings

    & Other Stories

    Why it looks expensive: A statement earring that glistens under the light paired with a chic outfit alludes to the idea that your ear candy is anything but designer.

    Editor's note: Everyday earrings that look expensive are a must for any fashion fanatic. These chunky drop chain earrings will instantly elevate any outfit to all new heights. Perfect on their own or added to an existing ear stack, the bolder the better I say.

  • Leather Shoulder Bag

    Arket

    Why it looks expensive: This satchel-esque mini bag is understated enough to look like luxury.

    Editor's note: Arket is known for making some of the best bags on the high street and this retro-inspired leather number is no different. I would wear this bag with everything and anything and use it everyday.

  • Wide trousers

    H&M

    Why it looks expensive: These wide-legged slouchy trousers from H&M are giving major Sofia Richie Grainge vibes

    Editor's note: It’s about to get even colder so a classic pair of trousers is a wardrobe essential that you’ll find yourself reaching for almost every week. I would wear these with a cosy sweater and an oversized wool coat.

  • Basic High Neck Knit Sweater

    Zara

    Why it looks expensive: If paired with the right bottom, a stripe turtleneck is as chic as it gets.

    Editor's note: If my predictions are correct, stripes will soon be everywhere in the fashion world so get ahead of the trend and treat yourself to a cosy certified French girl layer. I would go all-out Parisian and style the sweater with high boots, a mini skirt and stockings and a red lip.

  • Cotton Lace Scrunchie

    Urban Outfitters

    Why it looks expensive: Made from quality cotton, the delicate lace this is a statement accessory that exudes luxury.

    Editor's note: If you’re a girls girl then you’ll understand the oversized scrunchie hype. I would be lying if I said I hadn’t already added this to my cart. 

  • Faux Leather Box Pleat Mini Skirt

    Stradivarius

    Why it looks expensive: Faux leather is just as luxe as the real deal, giving off the assumption that you're sporting designer.

    Editor's note: I for one am always on the hunt for the perfect mini skirt and I think this might just be it. This pleated faux leather is the perfect throw-on option for when you want to look cute like a Miu Miu girl. Easily dress it up with a black corset and boots, or down with an oversized jumper and Ugg boots.

  • Long knitted skirt

    Mango

    Why it looks expensive: This long knitted skirt is giving The Row in all the right ways.

    Editor's note: I love how there is also a matching long sleeve to make it into a set. This would look amazing with a sparkly pair of kitten heels and an oversized woollen scarf.

  • Pink Oversized Stripe Shirt

    River Island

    Why it looks expensive: This crisp cotton number is giving Ralph Lauren Hamptons Soccer mom in all the right ways.

    Editor's note: You can never go wrong with a classic button-up shirt and this pink option with white cuffs and a collar is giving old luxury equestrian girly in all the right ways. This would look amazing over a silk slip or with a classic pair of blue jeans.

  • Ribbed one-shoulder top

    Monki

    Why it looks expensive: The true meaning of luxury is how versatile it can be. This particular one-shoulder can be dressed up or down and paired with other luxe items.

    Editor's note: When the dress code in the group chat is “jeans and a cute top” this is what they mean. This ribbed one-shoulder long sleeve in acid grey wash is giving Acne Studio energy and I for one am obsessed.

  • Melie Bianco Larissa Woven Faux-Leather Shoulder Bag

    Anthropologie

    Why it looks expensive: Made from supple leather, this intricately woven bag feels like it was made with tender loving care.

    Editor's note: We all know what bag this is a dupe for and we all love to see it. I love a fun-coloured bag because it easily elevates a monotone outfit and gives it a stylistic pop. I also love the size of this bag because it’s big enough to fit all the essentials but small enough to still wear as an evening bag if you need it.

  • Isla Blue & Gold Rectangular Hoop Earrings

    Oliver Bonas

    Why it looks expensive: Made from a two-toned gold and blue enamel, these could easily be mistaken for a luxe brand.

    Editor's note: As I said in my 2024 fashion bingo card prediction, colour is in this year. If you’re not one to go all out with bold hues, then these little blue hoops are the perfect stepping stone. I would wear these with any outfit that needs a hint of je ne sais quoi.

    Next

    Editor's note: A striking two-piece pyjamas ensemble, featuring a long-sleeve top and matching pants. The colours are rich and deep like wearing a piece of art. Could even wear out of the house during days of peak loungewear laziness.

    New Look

    Why it looks expensive: This chunky striped jumper exudes an air of luxury that can be attributed to the bold, contrasting black and white stripes which immediately give it a classic and timeless look, while the stylish collared V-neck adds a touch of sophistication that elevates it beyond a regular sweater.

    Editor's note: I'm loving this jumper because it marries comfort with chic design. The collar adds a preppy touch that makes it versatile enough to wear in a professional setting, yet it's cozy enough for a casual day out. The oversized fit because it gives off a modern, fashion-forward vibe.

  • High Rise Wide Leg Jeans

    Boden

    Why it looks expensive: A testament to what modern tailoring can achieve with denim. These wide leg pair from Boden have that elusive expensive look, with a rich indigo shade and stitching that hints at meticulous craftsmanship.

    Editor's note: I adore these jeans for their versatility. They can be dressed up with heels and a blazer for an upscale event or down with sneakers for a casual outing. It's a joy to find jeans that look this sophisticated while being so inherently comfortable.

  • Patent Midi Pencil Skirt

    Jigsaw

    Why it looks expensive: The unique Japanese patent faux leather material, has a luxe sheen and texture that catches the light beautifully, mimicking the sophistication of genuine leather while being a conscious choice. 

    Editor's note: This skirt is a celebration of form and function, a staple that doesn't just fit into my wardrobe but elevates it. Paired with a chunky knit and statement boots, it creates an ensemble that's both commanding and effortlessly chic.

  • Metallic midi skirt

    Mango

    Why it looks expensive: High-shine metallic finish, has become a hallmark of high-end fashion. The material mimics the textural appearance of a brushed metal surface, offering a visual complexity that simple fabrics can't achieve.

    Editor's note: The metallic moment isn’t over just yet and Mango knows this all too well. This pencil-shaped midi skirt would look amazing in winter with an oversized knit and blazer and even better in summer with a graphic t-shirt and lots of chunky jewellery.

  • High Heel Ankle Boots

    ARKET

    Why it looks expensive: These ankle boots boast big Bottega energy but on a budget. 

    Editor's note: f you’re in need of a pair of classic black boots but want something a little different, then these are it.

  • Wool Coat

    COS

    Why it looks expensive: The coat's luxuriousness is palpable, with its sumptuous, thick wool fabric that falls just so. Its oversized lapels and contrast piping exude craftsmanship, while the precise cut offers a timeless silhouette. 

    Editor's note: A classic that will forever last a lifetime, this woollen collared coat is as chic as it gets. Pair with your favourite winter boots and tights or with your comfy track pants for a casual day out.

  • Cream Embellished Collar Crop Jacket

    River Island

    Why it looks expensive: Could this be an ode to Chanel? We can’t be sure but we can assume that even Sofia Richie Grainge would approve of this style.

    Editor's note: Simple and elegant, this cropped woven jacket with a crystal-embellished collar would be a timeless addition to any wardrobe.

  • Denim Belted Trench Coat

    Anthropologie

    Why it looks expensive: The coat's rich, indigo denim is impeccably tailored, with striking contrast stitching. It's a luxurious take on a classic design, exuding effortless sophistication.

    Editor's note: The ultimate cool-girl trench has landed. Made from 100% cotton, this denim delight is the ultimate casual coat for those seeking a timeless wardrobe addition. I imagine this trench with either a pair of blue denim jeans (double denim is always in folks) with a hoodie, sneakers and a cap or as an added layer over a cream pantsuit.

  • Faux Leather Tailored Single Breasted Blazer

    M&S

    Why it looks expensive: The jacket's smooth, black leather exudes luxury, with a polished finish that's sleek and sophisticated. 

    Editor's note: Over the last few weeks we’ve been seeing a lot of faux leather coats (we’re looking at you Kylie) and can't help but feel that they might be sticking around. This blazer option from M&S is the ultimate quiet luxury piece and would look great paired over a knit turtleneck or silky slip dress.

  • Perl Lace Blouse Ivory

    Monsoon

    Why it looks expensive: The intricate lace detailing of this top is nothing short of exquisite, hinting at a labor of love and craftmanship.
