    The Scottish home of artist Richard Dawson, who died in 2021, has hit the market for £120,000 and the whole place is its own work of art.

    Boasting unique murals and paintings in every room, the house in Rhu, near Helensburgh on the west coast of Scotland, captures the magic of the late painter and gallery owner.

    The three-bedroom flat features more than a hint of Dawson's brushstrokes with hand-painted stairs, walls and ceilings ensuring the place is truly one of a kind. 

    Dawson, who owned a gallery in the area for 30 years, lived in the property until his death in September 2021.

    The painter spent three years studying artist Michelangelo's famous wall paintings after he saw a documentary on the Italian genius in 2014 and the products of Dawson's hard work can be enjoyed by the future owner.

    Dawson often spent months at a time perfecting each mural and used a magnifying glass to make sure every detail was right.

    And his painstaking efforts paid off. Dawson's larger works can be found adorning the walls of luxury stores, such as Harrods and Dior, as well as in swanky private homes in Switzerland, Belgium and the Channel Islands.
