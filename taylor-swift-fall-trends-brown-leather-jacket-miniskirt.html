    Taylor Swift celebrated the release of 1989 (Taylor's Version) in this fall's signature color palette.

    Last night, the singer was photographed while arriving at 4 Charles Prime Rib in Manhattan's West Village neighborhood, where she dined with her married friends Jack Antonoff and Margaret Qualley. For the outing, Swift wore a chic brown-on-brown look, comprised of a simple black top, a luxe russet corduroy miniskirt, and a boxy, chocolate-toned leather blazer by Madewell, which almost reached the hem of her skirt. She completed the autumnal look with a pair of shiny dark-red ankle boots with a block heel and a red-and-black box bag by Hunting Season.

    She accessorized with an array of gold jewelry, including two dainty chain necklaces adorned with gemstones, several rings, and mismatched hoop and cross-shaped earrings. The star wore her hair up with side-swept bangs, and her beauty look included chic black winged eyeliner and a bright red lip.

    Raymond Hall//Getty Images

    The "Anti-Hero" singer has spent the past fall showcasing her preppy street style all around New York City. On Thursday, she channeled Serena Van der Woodsen while heading to hang out with her celeb friends, including Ryan Reynolds and—Serena herself—Blake Lively. Swift dressed in a Stella McCartney oversize rugby top, skipping the pants and completing the outfit with a Ralph Lauren corduroy cap and Prada knee-high boots.

    And earlier this month, Swift channeled Princess Diana in an iteration of the eternal style star's go-to transitional fall outfit: the oversized crewneck sweatshirt/biker shorts combo with crew socks and white dad sneakers. The singer's outfit was comprised of an oversized Shania Twain t-shirt from Daydreamer, black biker shorts, green ruffled crew socks from Free People, chunky New Balance x Ganni sneakers, and a Still Here dad cap.
