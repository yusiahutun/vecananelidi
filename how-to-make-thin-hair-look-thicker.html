    How to make thin hair look thicker

    Albert Giordan for Harper's Bazaar Leave backcombing in the 80s - there are plenty of ways to make thin hair appear thicker without causing any damage to fragile strands. Here, some of London's leading hairstylists explain exactly how to supersize your strands.

    27 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Albert Giordan for Harper's Bazaar

    Leave backcombing in the 80s - there are plenty of ways to make thin hair appear thicker without causing any damage to fragile strands. Here, some of London's leading hairstylists explain exactly how to supersize your strands.

    1.Create optical illusions with colour

    The man known for bringing balayage to the UK, Jack Howard, says making thin hair look thicker with colour is really simple. The Paul Edmonds salon colourist explains, "The lighter the hair the thinner it can look, so adding dimension into it with darker pieces will add depth." How so? "This can be done with micro-balayage or baby lights [super subtle highlights], or clever placement of darker colours underneath the surface of the haircut can also really make the hair look thicker". Think of it like contouring. "It's an optical illusion, but it works."

    However, pay attention to the detail. Anna Short, colour director at the celebrity favourite salon Daniel Galvin, adds that when colouring finer hair types, less is more. "A few highlights can add texture to the hair, although too many can have the opposite effect and make it look even thinner." She reiterates that darker hair can naturally look thicker, "therefore – if the skin tone allows – going brunette may help hair to appear fuller".

    If you already have dark hair, "why not try several shades of chocolate rather than just one all over colour" advises Adam Reed, industry authority and founder of Percy & Reed. "Your hairdresser will be able to do this – a box colour wont!"

    2.Try discreet extensions and clever cuts

    Short also says you can cut your hair thicker. "Opting for a blunter cut over a more layered style also helps to keep the weight in the hair, making it look fuller," she advises. And don't forget regular trims – "to keep the ends strong and prevent it from becoming wispy".

    Of course, faking it with some hair extensions is always an option, and possibly "the most effective way to make thin hair look thicker," says Shannon Lewis, colour director at Neville Hair and Beauty.

    Fran Dixon, creative colourist at Hari's Salon on the King's Road, advises "try adding few extensions in at the sides." No one need know. "You don't need a full head of extensions, just a few to give instant thickness and guts." Ask your stylist for a consultation or colour-match your hair to some clip-ins to use at home.

    3.Products, products, products

    The power of products can't be denied, either. Reed recommends using prep products on fine hair "such as the L'Oreal Professionnel Tecni.ART Pli and the Full Volume Extra Mousse". Apply them before you dry your hair to get maximum body. Additionally, the appropriate use of a texturising product afterwards can create further fullness. Lewis says to try the L'Oreal Professionnel Tecni.ART Morning After Dust; a dry shampoo "which is great for giving volume to your natural hair."

    Don't forget that finer hair types are more susceptible to damage, "so good home care is essential," adds Short. "I recommend the Kerastase Volumifique range [a thickening effect shampoo, conditioner, mousse and spray] as it creates body in the hair while keeping it strong."

    Also ensure you're drying your hair without causing irreversible damage, by getting from wet to dry quickly, but without using high temperatures. Follow our drying guide to protect fine hair before focusing on body building.
