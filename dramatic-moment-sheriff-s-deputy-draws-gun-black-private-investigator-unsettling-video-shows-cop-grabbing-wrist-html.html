    California sheriff's deputy draws his gun at black private investigator in an video

    Private investigator Ken Sheppard was conducting surveillance in California A black private investigator captured a tense encounter with the police in a video that shows an officer pointing a gun at his head despite repeated reassurances that both his hands were visible.

    19 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Private investigator Ken Sheppard was conducting surveillance in California

    A black private investigator captured a tense encounter with the police in a video that shows an officer pointing a gun at his head despite repeated reassurances that both his hands were visible.

    Ken Sheppard was conducting surveillance in his SUV in Montrose, California, in March 2014, when a Los Angeles County Sheriff's Deputy Tai Plunkett approached.

    Despite Sheppard's multiple requests for a watch commander or police supervisor, the encounter escalated, with Plunkett first grabbing hold of Sheppard's wrist, before pointing a gun at his head after the PI removed an earpiece.

    Sheppard, who was not arrested, received a ticket for tinted windows and no license plate, although he did have a temporary registration and paper plates in place, according to journalist Jasmyne Cannick.

    Sheppard filed a civil lawsuit against six of the deputies in April 2015, but a jury of eight sided with the sheriff's department in September in a decision Sheppard hopes to appeal.

    Horrifying video footage captured by Sheppard shows Deputy Plunkett approaching his vehicle.

    Deputy Tai Plunkett approached the vehicle (left), asking Plunkett what he was doing. The situation grew tense after the deputy grabbed onto Sheppard's wrist

    Sheppard told the deputy both his hands were clearly visible and made multiple requests for a watch commander or police supervisor. But the encounter grew increasingly strained

    Plunkett eventually let go of the man, but when Sheppard lifted his arm to remove an earpiece, the deputy said: 'Do not reach' while raising his gun with his fingers on the hammer (pictured)

    Sheppard and Plunkett continue to argue, with the deputy continuing to aim his gun (pictured) at the man's head long after his fellow officers arrive and try to mediate

    While Plunkett's hands are not visible at the start of the video, Sheppard can be seen rolling down his windows and immediately stating: 'Holster your weapon'. 

    Sheppard repeats his request, stating 'You're pulling your weapon, so I'm telling you, "Holster your weapon". You've got nothing to see and my hands are clearly in sight.'

    Plunkett asks what Sheppard is doing, to which he responds that he's working.

    Sheppard fails to answer Plunkett's question as to whether he is a 'P.I.' and responds: 'Does it appear that I'm doing something illegal?'

    The situation escalates when Plunkett simultaneously grabs Plunkett's wrist and attempts to open the locked car door, while saying: 'Step out for me.'

    Sheppard remains seated in the car and asks the deputy to remove his hands while Plunkett continues to hold onto the private investigator while appearing to radio for backup.

    The encounter grows increasingly strained as Sheppard continues to hold on to the man's wrist with one hand, with his other hand on his gun.

    At one point, Sheppard says: 'Your hands are shaking. That makes me feel very nervous.'

    Plunkett eventually let go of the man, but when Sheppard lifted his arm to remove an earpiece, the deputy said: 'Do not reach. Do not f****** reach' while raising his gun with his fingers on the hammer.

    Plunkett eventually dropped his gun and Sheppard agreed to step out of his vehicle

    Sheppard, who was not arrested, received a ticket for tinted windows and no license plate, although he did have a temporary registration and paper plates in place

    Sheppard and Plunkett continued to argue, with the deputy still aiming his gun at the man's head long after his fellow officers arrived and tried to mediate.

    Plunkett eventually dropped his gun and Sheppard agreed to step out of his vehicle.

    The video emerged in late September, as police brutality has become a mounting source of strain around the country following the killing of numerous black people at the hands of the police.

    In an interview with Cannick (pictured), Sheppard explained that he removed the earpiece in order to hear the deputy

    In an interview with Cannick, Sheppard explained that he removed the earpiece in order to hear the deputy more clearly, since he had not register that he was asked to step out the first time.

    The private investigator accused Plunkett of failing to clearly state why he approached his car in the first place, adding the deputies ended up searching his car without a warrant.

    The jury ruled in favor of the sheriff's department, after Sheppard filed a lawsuit claiming the deputies violated his constitutional rights.

    Plunkett, who has served as an officer for 17 years, received a Congressional Badge of Bravery in 2014 after saving a woman who was stabbed.
