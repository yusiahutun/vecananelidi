    20 best black female sports reporters that you should watch

    Women in sports journalism are a force to be reckoned with. Like their male counterparts, female reporters bring in the energy, vigour, professionalism, and a little fun in sports. Black female sports reporters are especially putting in the work and proving to all that they deserve a seat at the table.

    Sports has been a unifying factor across the world. Seeing women get recognition in the industry is gratifying as, for the longest time, sports has been considered a male-only activity.

    Today, women host shows, start podcasts, and give evenhanded analysis and reporting. The black female sports analysts you see on your favourite sports channels did not get there by luck but through hard work and professionalism.

    20 famous black female sports reporters

    Do you enjoy watching female reporters? What draws you to them? Is it their analysis skills, how they argue their points, or do you happen to support the same teams as them?

    Fans often have favourite hosts and reporters depending on how they conduct themselves, their knowledge, and sometimes their physical appearance. The following are popular black female reporters, according to Spot Covery:

    1. Pam Oliver

    Pam Oliver is one of the longest-tenured sideline reporters in the NFL. She actively participated in sports while in high school, being a tennis, basketball, and even track and field player.

    This was many years ago when she was a student at Niceville High School in Niceville, Florida. In the year 1985, she started her broadcasting career at WALB in Albany, Georgia.

    The personality then worked in various other small and huge media houses before joining ESPN in 1993. She then went to work for FOX Sports in 1995 as a sideline reporter and later on TNT in 2005. She currently works as a senior correspondent and Fox NFL reporter.

    2. Arielle Chambers

    Arielle Chambers is that face that you cannot miss in sports journalism. The University of Oxford and North Carolina State University alumna has been vocal about promoting women in sports since she joined the industry.

    She hosts the WNBA show Her Time To Play and LA Sparks Rooted in LA. Chambers is also the brains behind Bleacher Report's HighlightHER, a platform dedicated to women in sports.

    3. Usher Komugisha

    Usher Komugisha has been growing her career ever since she joined the media. The Ugandan sports journalist and commentator was an athlete in school and played dodgeball, volleyball, netball, lawn tennis, cycling, golf, and track.

    She has worked in several international media houses and sports organizations, including ESPN, BBC, Al Jazeera, Sky Sports, and the International Basketball Federation (FIBA). She presently works as a sports reporter for Al Jazeera Media Network.

    4. Kristina Pink

    Kristina Pink works for Fox Sports for their NFL Coverage and reports for the Los Angeles Clippers. She started her career in 2008 as a sports anchor and reporter at WDBD-TV in Jackson, Mississippi, and moved to other media outlets before joining Fox Sports in 2012.

    She made a cameo in the 2019 sports and drama film High Flying Bird. Pink is one of fans’ favourite black female sports reporters in the NFL.

    5. Elle Duncan

    Lauren Elle Duncan is a broadcaster, host, reporter, and actress. She presently works for ESPN but has previously worked for media houses like WXIA-TV and New England Sports Network (NESN).

    Having been in the media for about two decades, Elle has received a couple of recognitions and accolades. They include Atlanta's Top 30 Under 30 by AUC Magazine and the 2011 Inspiring Women Award by Atlanta Dream.

    6. LaChina Robinson

    LaChina Robinson is one of the regular black female reporters on Fox Sports. She works for ESPN, Fox Sports 1, and FS South as a basketball analyst who calls college basketball games. ESPN hired her in 2009, and she has since contributed to other major sports stations.

    The personality loved sports from when she was a cheerleader to when she played sports in college, went on to become an assistant to the head coach and now has a career in sports broadcasting.

    7. Carol Tshabalala

    The South African-born sports broadcaster and producer is one of the most endearing voices at SuperSport International and Premier League Productions. She is the current host of the show Extra Time on SuperSport.

    Carol Tshabalala began her journey in sports broadcasting as an apprentice on the show Sports Buzz on South African Broadcasting Corporation when she was only 18 years old. The star reports and does commentation on football, boxing, and basketball, among other sports.

    8. Sage Steele

    Sage Marie Steele is one of the most colourful women in sports broadcasting. She debuted her career in TV sports in 1995 and has been on an upward trajectory since then.

    Her biggest recent job was co-hosting the 12 noon SportsCenter on ESPN. The 50-year-old has also hosted SportsCenter on the Road and different sporting events like the Super Bowl, The Masters, and NBA Countdown on ESPN and ABC during different periods in her career.

    9. Ros Gold Onwude

    Born Rosalyn Fatima Gold-Onwude, the American Nigerian broadcaster and a New York native is one of the best black female reporters to watch out for. The star played college basketball at Stanford and on the Nigeria national team.

    She got her first major gig in sports broadcasting in 2017 when she worked for Turner Sports. She had formerly worked as a sideline reporter for the Golden State Warriors. Gold Onwude joined ESPN in 2022, where she took the analyst role for the NBA on ESPN Radio.

    10. Taylor Rooks

    Taylor Rooks made her name in sports media at just 31 years old. You can watch her work on Thursday Night Football, Bleacher Report, and Turner Sports.

    She has been doing sports journalism since 2012, having started at Scout, where she covered Illinois football and basketball. She has also worked as a reporter, correspondent, and host at SportsNet New York and the Big Ten Network.

    11. Monica McNutt 

    Monica McNutt works as an analyst for ESPN. After getting her degree, she played high school basketball and worked as a kindergarten aide. She joined ESPN in 2019 as an analyst and shortly had other roles like SportsCenter and WNBA coverage added.

    The idea to join sports broadcast came while she was still a kindergarten aid, and she soon enrolled for her master's degree in journalism from The University of Maryland, where she graduated in 2013.

    12. Kimberley A. Martin

    Kimberly A. Martin has been a sports reporter for more than ten years. She joined ESPN in 2020, where her main role was to be an NFL reporter. Martin's other roles include analysis, writing, and hosting shows like Sunday NFL Countdown, SportsCenter, Around The Horn, and NFL Live.

    She also co-hosts ESPN's only all-female podcast, First Take Her Take. Prior to joining ESPN, Kimberley A. Martin worked for Yahoo Sports and The Washington Post. She is one of the most recognized black female sports analysts.

    13. Mimi Fawaz

    The Nigerian-Lebanese sports journalist and host has quite an impressive profile in sports media. The journalist is an alumna of the City University of London.

    Fawaz started her career as an intern at CNN and has since worked and contributed for BBC, Sky Sports, ESPN, and ITV Television networks. She now works as a show host and presenter on Focus on Africa, a program on BBC World News.

    14. Maria Taylor

    Suzette Maria Taylor has been in the limelight since 2009. She has covered college football, volleyball, the National Basketball Association, the National Football League, and men's and women's college basketball.

    Maria Taylor played basketball in high school and got an athletic scholarship to the University of Georgia, where she resumed playing volleyball and basketball. The personality works for NBC Sports and previously worked for ESPN and the SEC Network.

    15. Malika Andrews

    Talk of young black reporters on ESPN, and you have Malika Andrews, the host of NBA Today on ESPN. Before getting into mainstream media, the University of Portland graduate was a sports writer and editor for her school’s magazine, The Beacon.

    Andrews grew up as a fan of the Golden State Warriors and joined ESPN in 2018. Her roles at the sports giant include writer and sideline reporter. She has received a few recognitions, the most notable being named one of the Forbes 30 Under 30 in the sports industry for 2021

    16. Stephanie Ready

    Stephanie Ready is a sportscaster for NBA on TNT. Before getting into sportscasting, Ready was known for being the first female coach of a men's professional league team in 2001.

    She was the head coach at the NBA Development League, a position mostly reserved for men. Stephanie has been active in the sports industry since 1989 and is recognized for being a coach, seasoned sports analyst, and TV personality.

    17. Rhiannon Walker

    Rhiannon Walker started her career in sports in 2011 when she was a high school intern at USA TODAY. She has also been a reporting intern for The Baltimore Sun, The Oklahoman, and Houston Chronicle.

    Her first major role came in 2016 when she worked as an associate editor for ESPN. She worked for ESPN from 2016 to 2018, after which The Athletic hired her as a staff writer. The personality left The Athletic for Yahoo Sports in September 2023 to be a staff writer.

    18. Cari Champion

    The 48-year broadcaster and TV personality hosts The Cari Champion Show on Amazon Prime Video Sports Talk. Cari Champion started her career in the early 2000s and has since worked for reputable stations.

    She joined ESPN in 2012 and held various positions until her departure. Champion debuted on the Cari Champion show in November 2022.

    19. Christy Winters Scott

    Christy Winters-Scott had already made a name for herself when she was a student at the University of Maryland. She earned a full scholarship to play at the university and is considered one of the school's all-time top 10 players.

    After graduating, Winters-Scott went to play internationally for Switzerland and Italy. Today, she is a college basketball colour analyst for ESPN, FSN, The Big Ten Network, and NBC Sports Washington.

    20. Candance Buckner

    Candace Buckner graduated with a degree in journalism from the University of Missouri, which saw the start of her journey in sports. She was hired as a reporter for the Indianapolis Star and later on The Washington Post, where she currently is.

    Black female sports reporters - Final word

    Sports has tremendously grown over the years. The black female sports reporters who work in media organizations work hard to push for more females in the industry and create platforms and safe spaces where women can offer their services just as well as men.
