    Wrestling has always been at the forefront of creating global entertainment stars such as John Cena, The Rock, The Undertaker, Hulk Hogan, and Steve Austin, to name a few. However, it is not only the action in the ring that wrestling fans are passionate about. What goes on in the ring cannot be what it is without the narration of the WWE commentators. Some of the best WWE commentators have even developed larger personalities than the wrestlers.

    Commentators occupy an essential role in wrestling. Thanks to them, many fans have been schooled about the names of different wrestling moves such as the spear, blue thunder bomb, hurricanrana, and attitude adjustment. However, the best part is not their knowledge of these moves but the spectacularity of how they commentate.

    The best WWE commentators in pro wrestling history

    Who is the best WWE commentator? Jim Ross, Gorilla Monsoon, and Jonathan Coachman are some of the top analysts. Here is a list of the best wrestling commentators who died as well as those who are alive.

    1. Jim Ross

    Who is the greatest wrestling commentator of all time? The 69-year-old Jim Ross is arguably the best WWE commentator of all time. He had quite a lengthy career in WWE as a play-by-play commentator. He is famously referred to as Good ol' JR because of his infectious personality and wealth of experience.

    Jim made a colourful career not only as a commentator but also as an analyst and senior advisor. His career spans a long list of franchises such as the National Wrestling Alliance, World Championship Wrestling and World Wrestling Federation. During his time as a WWE commentator, he established himself as the company's voice for over a decade.

    2. Gorilla Monsoon

    Gorilla Monsoon, born Robert James Marella, was also a big deal in the wrestling world before his death on October 6th, 1999. He is among the wrestling commentators who died but will never be forgotten. In addition to being a play-by-play commentator, Monsoon was also a professional wrestler and booker.

    Inside the ring, he was viewed as a villainous super-heavyweight main eventer, but as a commentator, he was charming. In his peak years as a commentator, Monsoon was the voice of WWF. Towards the end of his career, he took up the on-screen role of WWF President for about two years.

    Monsoon served in various capacities because he had entered into an agreement with Vince McMahon. He sold him all his shares in exchange for a guarantee of lifetime employment.

    3. Joey Styles

    Joseph Carmine Bonsignore, alias Joey Styles, is a former American professional wrestling commentator. He is fondly remembered from his time in ECW and WWE and is one of the standout WWE commentators names. Before switching to wrestling as a commentator, he used to work as a full-time print advertising salesman in New York.

    As an announcer, Joey Styles was the first to call the maiden EWW One Night Stand event. Between 2005 and 2010, he was the official play-by-play commentator for Raw. However, he could not provide commentary for Wrestlemania 22 as the role was given to Jim Ross. This was because Vince McMahon preferred a storyteller kind of a commentator to pure play-by-play announcing, which was Styles' style.

    4. Jonathan Coachman

    The coachman of "The Coach" has had a distinguished career as a sports interviewer, pro wrestler, analyst and announcer. The part of his career that thrust him into the limelight was when he was part of WWE as a pre-show host for pay-per-views. He teamed up with Al Snow in a storyline feud against Jerry Lawler and Jim Ross, the Raw announce team.

    The Coach became the lead announcer of Raw in 2005 when Jim Ross was fired due to the actions of Steve Austin, who was his friend. Joey Styles would soon replace the coach as he had to settle with being the heel representative. He later rose through the ranks to be Raw’s Executive Assistant as well as Interim General Manager.

    5. Vince McMahon

    Even though he is famous for being the chairman and chief executive officer of WWE, Vince has also had successful stints as a commentator. He started as a commentator for his father when WWE was known as WWWF in the 1970s. After that, he served as the colour commentator and chief broadcaster until 1997.

    McMahon had always dreamt of becoming a wrestler, but his father did not let him, arguing that promoters were never meant to appear on the show but stay apart from their wrestlers. This means that the chance to be a commentator was a compromise he readily accepted because it allowed him to appear on TV.

    6. Bobby Heenan

    Any commentator who graced the wrestling world in style has to be Bobby Heenan, who was also a comedian, wrestler, and wrestling manager. He worked with the American Wrestling Association (AWA), World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment (WWE) and World Championship Wrestling. He died on September 17th, 2017, at the age of 72.

    His on-screen repartee with Gorilla Monsoon is perhaps the highlight of his career as a colour commentator. By the time he retired in the 2001 Wrestlemania X-Seven, Heenan had been a commentator in professional wrestling for seventeen years.

    7. Jerry Lawler

    Jerry O’Neil Lawler, also known as Jerry "The King" Lawler, is an American colour commentator and pro wrestler. He is actively signed to WWE as of 2021, where he is part of the current WWE commentators. He made his WWE debut in 1992 as a wrestler. He won several championships, such as World Heavyweight Championships.

    It was in 2001 when Vince McMahon introduced Lawler on Raw as a colour commentator replacing Paul Heyman. He worked with the likes of Jim Ross and Michael Cole. While most commentators' styles incorporate aspects of storytelling, Lawler preferred a reactive and vibrant style.

    8. Jesse Ventura

    Jesse Ventura 'The Body' is a former professional wrestler, commentator and politician. He was the 38th governor of Minnesota from 1999 to 2003 under the Reform Party ticket. During the Vietnam War, he was part of the U.S. Navy Underwater Demolition Team.

    He was a regular in the WWF/WWE for a long time, both as a performer and colour commentator. He was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame class of 2004. He also had his talk segment on WWF called "The Body Shop". In addition, he did colour commentary alongside the likes of Vince McMahon, Sammartino.

    9. Paul Heyman

    Paul Heyman will go down as one of the best wrestling managers/advocates having managed a record six WWE Champions. These are Kurt Angle, Big Show, Rob Van Dam, CM Punk, and Brock Lesnar. However, this does not negate his abilities on the microphone, as his style has always stood out.

    Heyman was recruited as a WWF commentator on Raw is War in 2001, replacing Jerry Lawler. Heyman used this opportunity to ignite a storyline rivalry with Jim Ross. However, he was still a commentator when he recreated ECW as a stable. He was later fired and replaced by Lawler.

    10. Eric Bischoff

    The former Executive Producer and Senior Vice President of World Championship Wrestling are among the greatest wrestling commentators of his generation. He is also an entrepreneur, TV producer, podcast host and professional wrestling booker. He once served as the on-screen manager of WWE's Raw brand.

    Before rising to be such an authority figure within wrestling circles, Bischoff worked as a commentator and a backstage interviewer. However, his ambition couldn't allow him to do it for long as he set his eyes on higher goals.

    11. Lance Russell

    Lance Russell may not have had the opportunity to be a commentator for WWE. However, he is still respected as a legend in the industry, especially by WWE commentators of the '80s. He used to be a commentator in the USWA and NWA territory of Memphis.

    From 1984 to 1987, he was a commentator in Continental Wrestling Association. During this time, he won all the annual Wrestling Observer's awards for best Television Announcer. He worked with notable announcers such as Bob Caudle and Gordon Solie.

    SLAM Wrestling referred to him as "one of the greatest wrestling announcers of all time". In addition, Steve Bowden, Jim Ross, and Mike Mooneyham had great words to speak of him.

    12. Matt Striker

    Matt Striker is one of the younger crops of wrestling commentators who have filled the shoes of former heavyweights like Gorilla Monsoon and Jim Ross. At 47 years old, Striker has accomplished many things, including being a professional wrestler, commentator, actor and former high school teacher.

    He trained with Johnny Rodz while still working full-time as a teacher and wrestling only during weekends and vacations. After wrestling for about three years, Matt Striker became a colour commentator for WWE's ECW and Smackdown brands from 2008. He stayed there for another five years before leaving WWE in 2013. He then worked on the independent circuit as well as Mexico's Lucha Underground.

    13. Tony Schiavone

    He is an American sports announcer, pro wrestling commentator and podcast host. As of 2021, he works with All Elite Wrestling as the play-by-play commentator and senior producer. His long career has seen him work for WWF (now WWE), Jim Crockett Promotions, Major League Wrestling, and World Championship Wrestling.

    In 2013 WWE noted that during the peak of the Monday Night War, Schiavone's voice was as crucial to the on-screen product of WCW as Jim Ross' Oklahoma growl was to WWE. The WWF will remember him for the one year from April 1989 in which he was the primary play-by-play announcer for both the SummerSlam 1989 and Royal Rumble 1990.

    14. John Bradshaw Layfield

    John Layfield is a businessman, TV personality, professional wrestler and commentator. He gained fame during the Attitude Era of WWF using the ring name Bradshaw. During that time, he managed to be a three-time WWF Tag Team Champion. He currently works as a financial commentator and a senior Vice President of Northeast Securities.

    It was when Bradshaw retired from wrestling in 2009 that he became an on-air commentator for WWE programming. However, he did not do for long as he stepped down in 2017 to focus more on humanitarian work.

    15. Michael Cole

    Michael Cole was a journalist before becoming a wrestling commentator. Currently, he is signed to WWE as the SmackDown brand play-by-play commentator. He also serves as the Vice President of Announcing. In his early years, he was a backstage interviewer and had to be patient for more than two years before getting the chance to work with the likes of Jim Ross and Kevin Kelly.

    In the early 2000’s he had a stint as a commentator for WWE Velocity. He shied away from storylines but only did them occasionally. He was the lead commentator for NXT in its first three seasons, but he started showing arrogance to wrestlers and fans alike, resulting in a feud with Jerry Lawler.

    The best WWE commentators in pro wrestling history will always stand out for the trademark ways they announced wrestling matches. No one can question the dedication of these commentators as they always brought out their A-game to add colour to the already exciting contests. Often, if fans remember a certain wrestling match, it will probably have to do with the performance of the commentator during that match.
