    Founder and creative director of L’Dezen jewellery, Payal Shah has found that good timing has helped her business grow from small beginnings to a jeweller to the stars.

    “My company L’Dezen officially turned 10 years old this year,” says Shah, who grew up in Hong Kong. “I started off with the idea of a manufacturing business, which wasn’t supposed to turn into a brand or label of any sort. The idea was to create pieces and then sell them to stores or brands that would then rename them under their own brand.”

    “However, because my pieces were so distinctive, people could tell they were mine. That’s when I realised I should start my own brand,” continues Shah.

    Shah is the second generation of jewellers in her family. “My father is a diamond merchant, and I learned a lot from him,” she says. While taking from the past, Shah also knows how to change with the times. Notably, she started a new, more affordable, ethically sourced jewellery brand, Psylish, launched during lockdown.

    “I’m doing more classical everyday wear – which was started as a Covid-19 baby – something comfortable with a lot more of a classic stance,” she says.

    “People around me wanted to have jewellery that they could sleep in, wake up and go to the gym, and also wear with a black dress,” adds Shah, who describes her brand as “beautiful, quirky yet elegant”.

    For L’Dezen, she created a new accessory and clothing line, recently adding fun bracelets, necklaces and headbands.

    “I took inspiration from the jewellery that I created in my past collection, especially the bejewelled athleisure – it was all crystallised and just something that I saw that people would like during Covid-19,” she added.

    During L’Dezen’s early years, Shah received an admiring email from American actress Mindy Kaling asking Shah to style her for the Cannes Film Festival. Then, while back in Hong Kong after a trip, Shah recalls she was watching American Idol and noticed that Sharon Osbourne was wearing a pair of her earrings.

    “I see Sharon Osbourne in my earrings and I know they’re my earrings because they were one of my most distinctive pairs. Like America’s Got Talent today, the world was watching the show, it was the No 1 show and so I took a screenshot of it and put it on my Facebook just to see how much business it bought in,” says Shah.

    “And yes, people were more interested in learning about my brand.”

    Celebrities such as Rihanna, Sofia Vergara and Demi Lovato have also bought her pieces.

    Despite her busy schedule, Shah says she manages a regular fitness regime. “I love boxing and I love just going to the gym,” she says, admitting she has so much energy that if she did not go for a 5km run or boxing, she would not be able to sleep.

    A tip? “I juice every morning, which is something I cannot live without.”

    And she always has time for family. “As a family, we have lunch together – my mum, dad and brother.”

    Payal Shah’s preferred watch – a vintage Piaget

    “It was passed to me by my mother and was the first nice watch she bought after she moved to Hong Kong. It was a sign to show how she had adapted to a new city, learnt to run a business and was able to buy something so beautiful.”
