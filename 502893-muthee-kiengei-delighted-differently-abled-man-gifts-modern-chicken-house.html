    Muthee Kiengei Delighted as Differently Abled Man Gifts Him Modern Chicken House

    Preacher Muthee Kiengei has expressed appreciation for a differently abled man who visited him bearing gifts. On Monday, April 17, Kiengei posted numerous images alongside entrepreneur Francis Muiruri.

    05 Jul 2024, 00:00
    • Jesus Compassion Ministries founder Muthee Kiengei received a modern chicken coop from a differently abled man
    • Francis Muiruri's coops allow people to rear chickens in a small area and have a water dispensation device
    • Kenyans were impressed by the innovativeness behind the coops, with many saying that they would promote Muiruri’s business

    Preacher Muthee Kiengei has expressed appreciation for a differently abled man who visited him bearing gifts.

    Muthee Kiengei gets chicken coop

    On Monday, April 17, Kiengei posted numerous images alongside entrepreneur Francis Muiruri.

    Despite being unable to walk, Muiruri had set up a successful business selling modern chicken coops

    According to Kiengei, the businessman visited his Jesus Compassion Ministries church to offer him the coop.

    “He came and offered to reward me with a 64-chicken modern cage.”

    The preacher was touched by the gesture and decided to visit Muiruri and promote his business.

    “In appreciation, I decided to buy chicken from him. Today he brought all the chickens, and in fact, he decided to sell those that have just started laying eggs.”

    According to Kiengei, the coop could dispense water, and the eggs laid rolled down to a specific holding place.

    Kiengei marvelled at Muiruri’s innovation, writing:

    “Indeed, this is marvellous. Just a small space at home, and I will be collecting more than 64 eggs per day. You can call and engage him and learn more about chicken rearing in this modern cage.I now believe what he normally tells me. Rev Ben, I don't beg despite walking with crutches, I am abled differently.”

    Muthee Kiengei's church overflows

    Elsewhere TUKO.co.ke reported that Kiengei’s first mass at his new church was a success.

    A photo shared by the preacher showed around 50 vehicles parked outside the church on Sunday. The tent could not hold a large number of congregants, and some had to sit outside under the sun.

    Kiengei disclosed that they were working on extending the tent to accommodate everybody.
