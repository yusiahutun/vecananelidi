    Fantasia Barrino wowed in a black sheer dress while accessorizing with a sparkling lip piece on Sunday while attending the 2024 Grammy Awards in Los Angeles.

    The 39-year-old singer wore a gown by Vietnamese designer Nguyen Cong Tri that featured a draped neckline, upper thigh slit and sheer skirt upon arrival at Crypto.com Arena for the 66th Grammy Awards.

    The Color Purple star completed her look with black open-toed collar heels and also donned matching gloves.

    She accentuated her natural beauty with full smoky eye makeup and pink lipstick while accessorizing with Messika jewelry, including a sparkling diamond lip piece, bracelet and stud earrings. 

    Fantasia, who also carried a black handbag, was accompanied by her husband Kendall Taylor who looked sharp in a black tuxedo.

    Fantasia Barrino wowed in a black and sheer dress with a slit skirt on Sunday while attending the 2024 Grammy Awards in Los Angeles .

    Kendall sported a salt-and-pepper beard and accessorized with sunglasses.

    Fantasia rose to fame while winning season three of American Idol in 2004.

    She won the Grammy Award for Best R&B Vocal Performance in 2011 for her single Bitterwsweet from her third studio album, Back To Me.

    Bittersweet was the last song to win in that category as it was discontinued in 2011 as part of a major overhaul of Grammy categories.

    Fantasia was slated to perform at the Grammy Awards during the In Memoriam segment along with Stevie Wonder, Annie Lennox, Wendy & Lisa and Jon Batiste.

    She will honor Tina Turner who died in May 2023 at age 83.

    Fantasia during season three of American Idol sang the 1960 Ike & Tina Turner classic "A Fool in Love".

    Oprah Winfrey, 70, will introduce Fantasia who starred in the 2023 musical The Color Purple that Oprah co-produced.

    This year SZA tops the nominations with a total of nine nods, including Album of the Year, Record of the Year, and Song Of The Year. Taylor Swift follows close behind with six nominations, and will also be competing against SZA in the three aforementioned categories.

    The majority of Swift's nominations were for her acclaimed album Midnights and its single Anti-Hero, which earned some of the best reviews of her career.

    That track is up for Record of the Year, a category that honors the artists who recorded the song and its producers and engineers, as well as for the Song of the Year category, which honors songwriters. SZA's track Kill Bill has also been nominated in both categories.

    Swift has already made history in the Album of the Year category, currently tied for three previous wins with music legends Frank Sinatra, Paul Simon and Stevie Wonder. However, she is now poised to become the first-ever person to win the category four times if Midnights ends up victorious this year.

    Billie Eilish and Miley Cyrus are also both up for six awards. Eilish's nominations include Song and Record of the Year for her hit single from the Barbie soundtrack, What Was I Made For?

    Cyrus has the chance to win her first ever Grammy this year with her nominations.

    Her track Flowers is up for Record and Song of the Year, while her album Endless Summer Vacation has been nominated for Album of the Year.

    Olivia Rodrigo is another major name in competition for a trophy.

    Olivia - who has already won three Grammys - has been nominated for six awards this year, including Album Of The Year, Record Of The Year, and Song Of The Year.
