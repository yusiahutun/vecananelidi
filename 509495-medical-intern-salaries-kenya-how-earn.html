    After graduating from medical school, prospective doctors, nurses etc., serve as interns for more practical experience. Interns work under experienced doctors who supervise their work and guide them. They assist in procedures, managing patients in emergencies and evaluating patients. But do medical students get paid during internship in Kenya? If yes, what are the medical intern salaries in Kenya?

    A medical career means job stability because the world needs more medical practitioners. In addition, medical staffs enjoy high salaries or at least higher than average because of the importance of their job and the increased demand for qualified professionals.

    Highest-paid medical interns in Kenya

    Most people choose the healthcare field to make a tremendous meaningful impact on people's lives. But if you can also create wealth, isn't that a win-win? All medical jobs pay well, but a few extremely high-paying exceptions exist.

    The highest-paid interns in Kenya are anesthesiologists, surgeons, gynecologists' and obstetricians, pediatricians and physicians. However, details about their salaries haven't been disclosed.

    How much are medical interns paid in Kenya?

    A medical internship is usually a one-year program for medical students. It exposes the selected interns to various specialities such as pathology, paediatrics, emergency medicine, and gynaecology. It also helps to develop their network, resourcefulness and confidence.

    What is the salary of an intern doctor? The average salary of an intern doctor is Ksh. 15,000 t0 Ksh. 80,000. But medical intern salaries per month depend on the hospital and other factors. Those interning in high-end private hospitals such as Nairobi Hospital and Aga Khan Hospital will get better pay than those in small private or public hospitals.

    Nurse-intern salary in Kenya

    Nursing is among the most in-demand courses in Kenya, and out there; it's not hard to see why. Nurses help patients in their most vulnerable and lowest moments and support them throughout recovery. Another great thing about nursing is reliable income and job stability, even during brutal economic crashes.

    However, most nurses (rightly complain) that their salary doesn’t match all the night shifts, stress and overwork. Overall, professional/ experienced nurses in Kenya earn modest salaries.

    What about nurse interns? There's no information about nurse-intern salaries in Kenya. Typically, Interns in Kenya earn approximately between Ksh. 15,000 to Ksh. 80,000. This means a nurse-intern will earn within this range, depending on the hospital. If it is a small hospital in rural parts of Kenya, the pay can be as low as Ksh. 10,000.

    Do medical students get paid during internship in Kenya?

    According to Kenya's labor law, all interns, including medical interns, should receive monthly stipends. That’s because interns also contribute to the organizations' workforce by providing apprentices and professional skills.

    In addition, interns too have needs such as food, housing, transport and more. The government understands this, so it passed the bill supporting interns' compensation.
