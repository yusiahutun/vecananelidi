    Paul Stanley is a singer and songwriter who became famous as a member of the rock band Kiss. He began his career over five decades ago and has earned his place on the list of the most important metal singers of all time. In addition to his career with Kiss, which has had a major impact on his musical career, he has also done some work as a solo artist.

    Paul Stanley – Biography

    Although most people know the singer as Paul Stanley, he was actually born as Stanley Bert Eisen on January 20, 1952, in Manhattan, New York City. He came from a Jewish family and grew up alongside a younger sister, Julia.

    What is interesting for such a famous singer is that he grew up with a deformed ear, which made it difficult for him to understand where sounds come from, or even to hear very well in a noisy environment. However, he loved music and started singing when he was a small child. At the age of seven, he already had his first guitar.

    He began his training at PS 98 before his family moved to Kew Gardens at the age of eight. While the music was his first love, he also had a talent for graphic arts. He went to the High School of Music & Art in New York, where he graduated in 1970.

    The singer began his career with a local band, Rainbow. In the 1970s he joined other bands, including Wicked Lester, before finally settling into Kiss, which he formed in 1973 with Gene Simmons, Ace Frehley, and Peter Criss. He released over 20 albums with the band and won many awards.

    Stanley has not made many recordings as a solo singer, but he has been able to release two albums: Paul Stanley (1978) and Live to Win (2006). He also released the live album One Live Kiss in 2008.
