    The ever-busy Prince Charles and Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall are enjoying some well-deserved time off. On Monday, March 18, the two were spotted hitting the beach in Barbados during a break from their 12-day Caribbean tour. The Prince of Wales and Camilla have never looked happier and more relaxed than when they basked in the sun and went for a swim into the gorgeous blue ocean.

    Charles, 70, and Camilla, 71, didn’t have much time in Barbados as their schedule was packed with royal engagements. After the brief respite, the couple returned to their royal responsibilities with a visit to the oldest synagogue in the Western Hemisphere in Bridgetown — the capital city of the small Caribbean island — on the morning of Tuesday, March 19. There, Charles and Camilla viewed a demonstration of how the island is equipped in the event of a hurricane.

    This tour first began when the pair landed in St. Lucia on Sunday, March 17. Throughout the rest of their Caribbean tour, Charles and Camilla will also visit St. Vincent and the Grenadines, St. Kitts and Nevis, Grenada and the Cayman Islands.
