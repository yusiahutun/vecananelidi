    Kelly Monaco Wiki/Bio, Modeling, Acting Career

    Kelly Monaco, an American of white origin, was born on 23 May 1976 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA, as the daughter of Albert “Al” and Camina Monaco. She is the third child of five daughters, and her siblings are Marissa, Amber, Carmina, and Christine in that order.

    Monaco began her acting career with acting lessons at Pocono Mountain High School after the family moved to Poconos. After this time she got a job as a lifeguard in one of the local resorts where she worked for a while before studying for two years at Northampton Community College.

    Her breakthrough came when Monaco sent her photo to Playboy in 1996 after a career in model making. The following year, 1997, she was named playmate of the month in April 1997. She also continued to work with Playmate in several special roles.

    Monaco’s first role on television was in the Baywatch drama series, which ran from 1997 to 1998. She also had some roles in movies like BASEketball, Mumford, and Idle Hands.

    In September 2003 she joined the cast of the General Hospital, the ABC Soap Opera. She played Samantha McCall. This happened after the less popular Port Charles – another ABC soap opera in which she played Olivia “Livvie” Locke Morley – was finished.

    In 2003 and 2006 she was nominated for the Daytime Emmy Award as Outstanding Supporting Actress for her role in Port Charles and Outstanding Lead Actress for her role in General Hospital.

    In 2006, Monaco was one of the two co-organizers of the thirtieth annual Daytime Emmy, alongside Tom Bergeron, who also hosts Dancing with the Stars.

    Kelly Monaco also worked with Donald Trump when Trump and Paula Shugart selected her as one of the judges for the Miss USA 2009 election.

    Dirty Soap, a reality TV series on E! premiered on September 25, 2011, and also starred Monaco.
