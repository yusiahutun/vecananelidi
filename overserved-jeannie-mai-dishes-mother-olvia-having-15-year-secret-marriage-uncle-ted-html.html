    Overserved: Jeannie Mai dishes about her mother Olivia having 15-year secret marriage to 'Uncle Ted'

    Jeannie Mai Jenkins discussed her mother's 15-year secret marriage on Thursday's episode of Overserved with Lisa Vanderpump. The 42-year-old stylist and co-host of The Real only found out about Olivia Tutram Mai's wedding two years ago.

    03 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Jeannie Mai Jenkins discussed her mother's 15-year secret marriage on Thursday's episode of Overserved with Lisa Vanderpump.

    The 42-year-old stylist and co-host of The Real only found out about Olivia Tutram Mai's wedding two years ago.

    What's more, she might never have known about it if she hadn't busted Olivia's husband coming out of her room in the middle of the night.

    Secret marriage: Jeannie Mai Jenkins discussed her mother's 15-year secret marriage on Thursday's episode of Overserved with Lisa Vanderpump

    She explained how it all went down to Lisa, 60, and fellow guest Meagan Good, 39.

    'One day, my mom's friend ''Uncle Ted'' asks me if he can come to L.A. to hang out,' Jeannie said. 'I'm like, ''Of course, come and stay at my house.'' I put Uncle Ted in another bedroom, Mama Mai is staying upstairs. In the middle of the night, I walk by my mom's room, I see Uncle Ted tip-toeing out of her room and to the restroom.'

    'He's shagging your mother and you didn't know!' Lisa cried.

    'I thought the same thing,' Jeannie confessed. 'So I confronted my mom and she looked at Uncle Ted the next morning, and she said, ''You tell her! You tell her! We keep secret for long time now.''

    Recent revelation: The 42-year-old stylist and co-host of The Real only found out about Olivia Tutram Mai's wedding two years ago

    'What's the secret?' Lisa asked.

    'Well, you know, I'm not really Uncle Ted; I'm your mom's husband,' Jeannie recounted, speaking as Ted.

    She explained that her mother had met Ted on an 'Asian Tinder,' dated and married him, then he met the family, but she didn't want to tell Jeannie they were wed.

    Online dating: Jeannie explained that her mother had met Ted on an 'Asian Tinder,' dated and married him, then he met the family, but she didn't want to tell Jeannie they were wed

    'For how many years?' Lisa asked.

    'For like, 15 years!' Jeannie replied, still in disbelief. 'I found out two years ago!'

    The former DWTS contestant said Olivia gave birth to her at age 17 and separated from her father when Jeannie was 16 years old, divorcing at some point later.

    Longtime secret: 'For like, 15 years!' Jeannie said of the longtime secret marriage, still in disbelief. 'I found out two years ago!'

    Having had a child so young, Olivia was revisiting her youth as she neared 60, regularly partying and traveling the world.

    Jeannie videochatted with Olivia and showed Lisa her outrageous hairdo, platform heels and miniskirt; naturally, she was at a party, too.

    For her part, Jeannie was celebrating the latest themed evening at Lisa's Beverly Hills home.

    The hostess: The British restaurateur welcomed her guests to her Villa Rosa estate

    For 'A Night in Morocco,' there was a feast, starting with a pomegranate and rose citrus martini, and dates stuffed with cheese and almonds.

    Jeannie then spoke about her engagement to rapper Jeezy, 43, showing off a huge diamond ring.

    She told the women that he had intended to propose to her in Vietnam, where her family was from, but because of the pandemic ended up creating a Vietnamese scene in their condo instead—full of candles, authentic lanterns and Vietnamese food.

    Happy couple: Jeannie then spoke about her engagement to rapper Jeezy, 43, showing off a huge diamond ring

    'He led me to the fireplace and got down on one knee, and said some beautiful things to me, and next thing I knew I was screaming ''yes!'',' Jeannie related.

    She explained that he'd said how beautiful it was to find someone who could love him and also help him grow in the way he needed to, and that she felt the same way.

    Jeannie and Jeezy were married on March 27, 2021; on Tuesday, she announced on The Real that she had officially changed her last name to his: Jenkins.

    Meagan, who'd been married nearly nine years to preacher Devon Franklin, 43, called him 'the best thing that's ever happened to me.' 

    Best thing: Meagan, who'd been married nearly nine years to preacher Devon Franklin, 43, called him 'the best thing that's ever happened to me'

    The trio then played Fountain Pong, the rules of which were that anyone who bounced a ping-pong ball into Lisa's fountain got to issue a dare to someone else.

    Lisa and Meagan were dared to chug alcohol. 

    Jeannie and Meagan were challenged to twerk.

    Fun game: The trio then played Fountain Pong, the rules of which were that anyone who bounced a ping-pong ball into Lisa's fountain got to issue a dare to someone else

    For dinner they had a chicken tagine with spicy couscous and cauliflower, and were joined by Lisa's husband Ken Todd, 75, and their daughter Pandora Todd, 34.

    Meagan admired Lisa's dogs and confessed that she once had two tiny marmoset monkeys named Esther and Rocky.

    'If I had to leave the house or put them in their cage, they would get mad and throw their poop at the wall,' she lamented.

    Pet monkeys: Meagan admired Lisa's dogs and confessed that she once had two tiny marmoset monkeys named Esther and Rocky

    The group then enjoyed a spiked Moroccan mint tea and played Lisa's card game Get Pumped, answering salacious questions.

    They all agreed that they would rather walk in on someone masturbating—a word Lisa couldn't bring herself to say—instead of being caught themselves.

    Lisa was the only one who liked to have sex with the lights off, she noted for the next card.
