    A genogram is more than just a family tree. It visually represents your lineage, along with your family's history of physical and mental health.

    The good thing is, you don't need to use a pen and paper to create a genogram. There are several tools available that you can use to create a genogram of any size. Here's a list of the top genogram makers you must try.

    1. Canva

    Canva is a free online tool that lets you create a genogram to display your complete family history. With over 100 customizable templates, making a genogram with a visual map of your lineage is just a few clicks away.

    Related: Tips to Get the Most Out of Designing on Canva

    While creating your genogram, you can use the color and theme of your choice to make it look more attractive. Besides adjusting the size of your genogram, the app allows you to add content from its collection of photos, images, shapes, icons, and even stickers.

    After creating the genogram, you can download it in PDF, JPEG, or PNG format. Genograms made in Canva are also sharable on Facebook or via email.

    2. WinGeno

    WinGeno is a free genogram maker with a minimalist interface. If you'd like to create a genogram from scratch and without templates, this tool is for you. In case you do need some extra help, however, you can take suggestions from the tool by heading to the Help Topics section on the menu.

    Each of its features is represented by an icon for easy identification. Thus, you can find and add what you need with a quick click. Its dedicated window for editing lets you edit the text in a hassle-free way. You can export the genogram in multiple file formats, like PDF, XLSX, PPT, and more.

    Download: WinGeno for Windows, Mac, and Linux (Free)

    3. Progeny

    Progeny is another genogram creator that assists you in keeping track of your family history. Thanks to its drag and drop interface, you can easily make a genogram with this tool. It also lets you customize your genogram's spacing, size, and style.

    Apart from adding and editing individuals and relationships, you can also modify five custom data fields for each individual.

    You can save the genogram file as an image on your computer, share it with others, or print it out. However, some of the features of this tool are more complex, and you may need to re-create the whole genogram to make any major changes.

    4. MyDraw

    MyDraw is an advanced diagram maker that's also suitable for making genograms. It offers premade layouts, as well as tools to help you format and customize your text. All you need to do is drag your preferred elements into the editing panel.

    You can choose from its readymade genogram templates to get started. The app is compatible with Microsoft Visio, therefore you can import and export different Visio format files. After your genogram is ready, you can save it in several different file formats, including PDF, SVG, JPEG, BMP, and more.

    Download: MyDraw for Windows and Mac (Free)

    5. Family Echo

    If you need to build an interactive genogram, Family Echo is the tool for you. It's perfect for all users, whether you're experienced or new to creating genograms.

    Here, you can add information like an individual's name, place of birth, date of death, and more. As you include information about your family members, this tool automatically adds nodes. The app also lets you import data from GEDCOM or FamilyScript.

    Related: The Best Free Family Tree Templates for Microsoft Word and Excel

    After completing it, you can print it to keep a hard copy. The data you enter in this app is secure, and only those who have access to it can see it.

    6. Creately

    With Creately, you can visualize your family lineage and medical history with minimal effort. The tool also allows for collaboration, letting you work on your family genogram with other family members. Any member can make changes to it, thus making the whole process highly efficient.

    Creately offers a collection of professionally designed shapes that can speed up your drawing process. It has a user-friendly, drag-and-drop interface, making it easy for newbies to create a genogram. You can also take advantage of the autosave option to recover your progress in case your browser crashes or the power goes out.

    You can use Creately for free with up to three documents, but you'll have to upgrade if you want to create more charts.

    7. EdrawMax

    If you want to make the process of creating a genogram a breeze, EdrawMax is the tool for you. This app allows you to include a range of information about all your family members. It also supports real-time collaboration with other members of your family.

    With this app, you can keep all the heredity data aligned and organized perfectly. It offers built-in templates and symbols for making professional-grade genograms. You can even edit every detail with the help of its dynamic toolkits. When finished, you can export your genogram in a variety of formats, such as PPT, PNG, JPG, and PDF.

    While you can use EdrawMax for free in your browser, you'll have to pay a subscription fee if you want to access all of its features and templates.

    8. GenoPro

    This Windows-based genogram maker lets you display the relationships between the members of your family. It divides the family into several branches, making it organized and easy to understand. One cool feature is its problem spotter that runs in the background; this allows it to track any errors in your family tree.

    You can navigate through the branches with hyperlink connections. Before printing the genogram, you can modify the layout.

    Download: GenoPro for Windows ($49, free trial available)

    9. GitMind

    Are you looking for an easy-to-use genogram maker that you can access from anywhere? Try GitMind, the free web-based chart-making app. Every option and feature you need to make an informative and complete genogram is available in this robust tool.

    Navigation in this app is seamless, which means you can use your creativity and family history to create a genogram without any trouble. GitMind lets you customize the look of your genogram, including the text, color, theme, and more.

    It also supports collaboration, so you can have other family members contribute as well. And if you want to use the app while you're out and about, you can also try its mobile version.

    Download: GitMind for Android | iOS (Free)

    10. Visual Paradigm

    Visual Paradigm is a professional genogram tool that runs on cloud-based technology. This means that you can draw and access your genogram from any device and browser 24/7. This collaborative drawing tool also allows you to add comments and discuss them with other users.

    Here, you get to choose from more than 1,700 professionally designed templates. It also provides you with a rich collection of genogram tools and symbols. To get started, simply head to Visual Paradigm, click the Create a Genogram button, choose your preferred template, and tailor it to your family.

    You can share your genogram with others in file formats like PNG, JPG, SVG, GIF, and even PDF. Also, you can embed it in Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint projects.

    A Genogram Is an Important Family Document

    Genograms are used to analyze hereditary factors, family relationships, and psychological patterns. Most people also create a visual genogram of their families to keep track of their lineage. You can try any of the discussed apps to make a beautiful and informative genogram of your family.
