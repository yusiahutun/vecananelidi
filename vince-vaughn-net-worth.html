    The career of Vince Vaughn

    • Vince Vaughn got an opportunity to appear in a television commercial for Chevrolet in the year 1988. After that, he packed off and left for Hollywood to become an actor.

    Movies and TV Shows

    • He began his acting career in television in the year 1989 by appearing in episodes of the series China Beach and 21 Jump Street.
    • He made his movie debut in 1993 playing Jamie O’Hare in the sports film Rudy. After that, Vince appeared alongside Jon Favreau in the 1996 comedy-drama film Swingers.
    • The famous director Steven Spielberg was impressed by his performance in Swingers and cast him in the movie The Lost World: Jurassic Park in 1997.

    • Vince got several movie offers in 1998: A Cool, Dry Place, Clay Pigeons, and Psycho.
    • Unfortunately, none of these films performed well that’s why he took a short break from acting. He returned to action in the year 2000 with the psychological thriller The Cell.
    • After that, he starred along with Luke Wilson and Will Ferrell in the 2003 comedy film Old School.
    • In 2005, Vince appeared in the romantic comedy Wedding Crashers. Later, he was cast opposite Jennifer Aniston in the 2006 romantic comedy-drama The Break-Up.

    His Career as Screenwriter and Producer

    In the year 2001, he co-produced and starred in the comedy Made which was written and directed by his friend Jon Favreau. Vince produced a lot of movies during the later part of the decade, the major ones are Fred Claus (2007), Four Christmases (2008), and Couples Retreat (2009). Also, sis more recent movies include The Dilemma (2011), Lay the Favorite (2012), and The Internship (2013).

    Awards and Achievements

    Vince won the MTV Movie Award for Best On-Screen Team for Wedding Crashers in 2006 which was shared with Owen Wilson. He along with Jennifer Aniston was awarded the Teen Choice Movie Award for Choice Chemistry for The Break-Up in the year 2006.
