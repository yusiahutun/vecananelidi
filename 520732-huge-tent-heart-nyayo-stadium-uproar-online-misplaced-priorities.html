    Huge Tent at the Heart of Nyayo Stadium Causes Uproar Online: &quot;Misplaced Priorities&quot;

    • Kenyans expressed their fears that at the end of the reported church event, the playing surface would be rendered useless for some time
    • A section of Kenyans also supported the move, arguing that even political rallies have been hosted there, and nobody complained

    Nairobi - Kenyans have, in recent years, developed a soft spot for sporting activities as it has shown to be an alternative source of income.

    Parents have been keen on supporting their children's desires to pursue careers in sports compared to previous years.

    Sporting facilities have remained a challenge to most as the country grapples with a lack of stadiums, among other sports infrastructure.

    The iconic Nyayo stadium that stands tall in Nairobi has been at the centre of most sporting events in Kenya.

    The 45,000-capacity stadium opened in 1983 has been Kenya's to-go facility alongside Kasarani Stadium.

    Tent in Nyayo stadium

    On Monday, September 11, images of a massive tent at the centre of the facility left Kenyans fuming over who authorised the event.

    In the photos, hundreds of chairs could be seen well-arranged ahead of what looked to be a church event.

    Most who reacted to the image were cross about what the event means to the stadium that has undergone numerous closures to give time for the grass to sprout.

    However, a section of Kenyans saw no issue with the event as they accused those complaining of hypocrisy, arguing that they never complained when parties held political rallies in the stadiums.

    Coupled with the huge crowd expected to step on the delicate playing surface, the grass will certainly not receive adequate sunlight during the period of the event.

    How did Kenyans respond to the development

    TUKO.co.ke highlighted some of the sentiments shared by Kenyans on the tent covering the playing surface.

    George Makambi:

    "This is a scam, absolute madness lack of visionary ideas as pertains to the only infrasturer approved by FIFA to host matches kindly rethink."

    Tom Khamasi:

    "Stupidity at it's best, we have an auditorium for such events at uhuru gardens rotting away because it was built by Uhuru Kenyatta hence not fit for use by the current government and its honchos."

    Shauriyako Makadara:

    "The problem with Kenya is that facilities are not used for their intended purpose. We pretend to understand football by watching the EPL yet we don't recognise that the most important part of the stadium is the pitch!!"

    Alexander Njiri Snr:

    "Last weekend we were told the stadium was closed for renovations.... what a serious renovation."

    Njoroge Par Trick:

    "As long as they paid for the venue,it's well part of the price is used for renovations and watering of the grass."

    Kuria announces bid to host 2027 AFCON

    Earlier, Investments, Trade and Industry Cabinet Secretary Moses Kuria announced Kenya's motive to bolster other East African counties in seeking to host the 2027 African Cup of Nations.

    Kuria marvelled at their engagement with United Kingdom's High Commissioner to Kenya Jane Marriott, who shared the rudiments of hosting such a prominent tournament as Afcon.

    "Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, DRC, Ethiopia and South Sudan will jointly bid to host the Africa Cup of Nations in 2027. Tonight we received valuable tips from the UK High Commissioner on the sidelines of the England-Wales WC Match," said Kuria.
