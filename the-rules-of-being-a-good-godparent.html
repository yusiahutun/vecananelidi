    A godparent, according to the dictionary, is a person who takes responsibility for a child’s religious education - but in 2013, it’s less about religious fervor, and more about an extra pair of hands – think baby-sitting duty, a guaranteed guest at birthdays, and someone who might make an embarrassing speech about you on your 21st. It’s not a job to be taken lightly, that’s for sure.

    With that in mind, here’s our advice to Prince George’s godparents… 

    Always buy great presents. The godparent who neglects their charge’s birthday and/or Christmas is a woeful guardian indeed. It is the solemn duty of the godfather and godmother to always supply an excellent present twice a year and until at least the 21st birthday. Anything less is downright diabolical – after all, what is a godparent if not a guaranteed source of presents? Oh, and if in doubt, cash is always better than gift vouchers.

    Be a power godparent. Exert your influence for the greater good. Be that godparent all the other children wish they had. This does mean using every tenuous contact you’ve ever made to get your godchild an internship/job/holiday.

    Be fun. Look on your godchild as your protégé – a ready-made mini-me who you can mold throughout their lives. Get them interested in your hobbies. Have a laugh together. Take them for their first drink when they're 18. You want to be that cool relative they go to for advice/lifts to parties/to escape the parents. (NB. If Prince Harry is your fellow godparent, you’re going to have to go seriously rogue to out-fun him). 

    Know your place. They’ve got parents to discipline them, grandparents to mollycoddle them, and siblings to annoy them. Never take sides. And never get involved in familial arguments. It’ll only end in tears. 

    Actually take it seriously. If we're honest, godparents are fast becoming a thing of the past, but we must maintain this great tradition if we're to prosper as a nation. So for goodness sake, take it seriously. Do all the above things, and do it with a smile on your face and a song in your heart. Set an example!
