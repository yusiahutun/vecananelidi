    Double joy for Lisa Marie Presley as she gives birth to twin girls

    By Daily Mail Reporter
    Updated: 18:43 EST, 11 October 2008

    View
    comments


    A little tired: First photos of a pregnant Lisa Marie Presley

    Elvis's daughter Lisa Marie Presley has given birth to twin girls by Caesarean section.

    Her musician husband Michael Lockwood, 47, was by the 40-year-old singer’s side ‘the whole time’, at Los Robles Hospital and Medical Center in Thousand Oaks, California, said a friend of the family.

    One baby weighed 5 pounds, 15 ounces and the other came in at 5 pounds, 2 ounces.

    The girl's names are yet to be released.

    Lisa Marie’s two older children, Riley, 21, and Ben, 16, from her first marriage to Danny Keough, were also present for the twins’ arrival.

    The family friend added: ‘Lisa Marie is feeling good, though a little tired. She is so happy and Michael is ecstatic.

    'He’s wanted to be a dad for so long. This is his dream come true.

    'The birth went along without a hitch. There was no drama.'

    ‘The babies are healthy and beautiful. Ben was seen pushing them down the hall in their cart and cooing to them.’

    Proud parents: Lisa Marie Presley and her husband Michael Lockwood are said to be overjoyed with the birth of their twin girls

    The singer, 40, had said on her web page before the babies were born that she and her husband were 'beyond thrilled and blessed, elated and saturated in happiness.'

    The girls are the first children together for Presley and Lockwood.

    Presley's mother, Priscilla, had been the one to initially confirm the twins.

    'She wanted it really to be kept a secret for a long time,' Priscilla said at the time.

    'And I think women should, because they should be able to announce what they're having and when it's time to make the announcement.'
