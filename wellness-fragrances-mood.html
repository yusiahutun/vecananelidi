    What are functional fragrances? Can wellness perfumes enhance your energy?

    The rise of the wellness industry shows no signs of slowing. It is now worth 1.5 trillion dollars globally, according to a report by McKinsey, and its a sector growing by up to 10 per cent a year.

    02 Apr 2024, 00:00

    The rise of the wellness industry shows no signs of slowing. It is now worth 1.5 trillion dollars globally, according to a report by McKinsey, and it’s a sector growing by up to 10 per cent a year.

    Of course, this was exacerbated by the pandemic and, more recently, a cost-of-living crisis, where a new, conscious consumer emerged; one who's intent on buying products that do more with less. In the beauty industry specifically, this translates to consumers wanting products that are not only effective but meaningful. Are they 'clean'? Are they sustainable? Do they align with the wearers' values? These elements have played out across both skincare and make-up for some time – but one of the final frontiers was fragrance.

    Now, a new generation of perfumes has emerged. These, grouped as 'functional fragrances', or more widely, 'wellness fragrances', aim to influence our mood at a more scientific or energetic level – as well as simply smelling wonderful.

    “Functional fragrances go beyond the traditional concept of perfumes or colognes that are primarily designed for scent aesthetics," explains Keelan Doyle, the co-founder of Øthers, a brand utilising science-backed olfactory and sound technology, with two mood-altering functional fragrances linked to a multi-sensory wellness app – together intended to positively impact your state of being. "Instead, functional fragrances are formulated with the intention of influencing the user's emotions, mood, or cognitive state. They combine the power of scent with certain active ingredients to elicit specific physiological or psychological responses. Mainly stress reduction and mood enhancement – our scents are designed to prime the physiological and psychological to be in the optimal state for mindful practises."

    An early adopter in this sector is The Nue Co., which was founded in 2017 to address ineffective supplementation and change the world of supplements into a luxury experience. “We launched our original Functional Fragrance back in 2018, and at that point it really was something very new,” says Nue Co.’s VP of product, Flo Glendenning. “It stemmed from our belief that supplements don't need to take the form of pills, powders and tinctures, but looking at our health as an ecosystem. The reality is that a capsule takes, on average, between 20 and 40 minutes to break down, depending on the shell. And a tincture takes about 20 minutes to absorb into the body. So, none of these offer immediate benefits, whereas your sense of smell, your olfactory system, is one of the fastest ways to impact our cognitive and emotional state.” On its original "instantly de-stressing supplement", they worked with the nose Frank Vogel, who also created the cult classic Santal 33 by Le Labo. The fragrance works as a supplement – alerting and activating different areas of the brain – without compromising on how good it smells.

    Now, across all of Nue Co. fragrances, "we are using patented neuroscience technology to tap into different parts of your brain to deliver those different benefits – from focus to energy," explains Glendenning. "The idea is that there are certain parts of your brain that respond to certain senses. Different families of scents make different parts of our brain react. For example, woody scents tend to be very calming for us, whereas citrus ones tend to stimulate a different part of our brain which acts as a form of invigoration – I like to think of it as like giving a shot of energy to a particular part of the brain. So when you're having one of those days where you can't really get your head in the game, you're just feeling a bit sluggish or you need a brain reset, using one of these products can really help facilitate that mind shift. For Mind Energy specifically, after 32 days of use, 83 per cent of people said they had increased levels of focus, and 72 per cent of people said they had increased productivity. And we weren't asking them to do anything additional aside from using the fragrance."

    Richard Phibbs

    Also elevating the link between mood and scent is newer fragrance brand Edeniste, founded by Audrey Semeraro. The range features six 'EDP actives', each one containing a patented 'de-stressing accord' plus six 'Lifeboosts', which targets specific needs such as 'Happiness' and 'Relax'. "We are the first to have combined the world of fine fragrance with leading neuroscientists’ research on the science of behaviour," Semeraro tells Bazaar. "Our fragrance formulas are based on over 40 years of research in the field of neuroscience on the effect of scent on emotion." Each scent is tested on consumers at the French National Centre for Scientific Research using various methodologies including fMRI to measure brain activity.

    Another brand that is also innovating in the wellness fragrance space is Vyrao, which launched in May 2021. Founded by Yasmin Sewell, the former vice president of creative at Farfetch, the perfume brand is more about conjuring up specific moods and emotions – all with added healing properties. Even the bottles themselves come complete with a charged diamond crystal. “My reason for launching Vyrao, and why it came to me, pre-pandemic in 2019, was because I genuinely felt that there was a shift in people's attitudes – how we connected with the brands and why we buy the things that we buy. After 25 years in fashion, I didn't think that something that just looked really cool or pretty was enough. It needed to be about what was going to make me love something, or truly connect,” says Sewell. She approached it as a wellbeing brand, over beauty. "It was more about energy," she says. Knowing that fragrance’s connection with sense and emotion and the shift it can create is so powerful, she took the brief of evoking certain emotions to the perfumer Lyn Harris (creator of Miller Harris), who created a line of five fragrances – from the hypnotic Witchy Woo to the fluid Free 00. They worked with classic perfume ingredients that were also known for their healing benefits. “We used waterlily because the essence brings you courage of heart, iris which is very cleansing, and bergamot for lifting and letting go,” explains Sewell.

    Elsewhere, menstrual cycle care brand FEWE offer a functional fragrance, too. Eau So Happy is specifically formulated to help ease hormonal mood swings while generally lifting the spirits. A key component in the formula is CBD, included to settle a frantic mood, while orange, neroli, vetiver and amber boost mindset and help instil calm.

    The brand says to use it whenever you need a lift for the day ahead. "Just take a deep breath, spray, hold in for four and breathe out for four," the website instructs.

    But perhaps the most enriching offering in the space is Øthers' unique propsition. While the brand only sells two fragrances (Red Skies, which triggers a more focused active mind, and Mystic Zingaro, which elicits a more relaxed state) its app fuses the benefits of scent – aromachology – with sound, technology and science "to biomechanically re-tune, bringing you back to centre," Doyle tells us. "Creating the brand, we’ve pulled from the insight and expertise of a radical group of olfactory experts, neuroscientists, breath-work revolutionaries, music, producers, and philosophers," and the very real synergy between scent and sound can be felt when blanketing one's self in the scent while listening to the app ritualistically.

    Courtesy of Øthers

    Naturally, it's not only the indie brands innovating in functional fragrances – even beauty giants like Estée Lauder are embracing the trend too, having launched a line of eight fragrances under the Luxury Collection range. For this, the brand commissioned an independent neurosensorial study to determine the emotional effects of each scent on the wearer. Blushing Sands, for example, claims to put one in a state of sensuality and relaxation.

    Ultimately, wellness fragrance is still a young market and, while more and more brands are veering into it, it’s not yet the norm for fragrance brands to consider additional properties to scents as standard. But with science and tech moving at a rapid pace, we expect plenty more to incorporate research-backed, mood-boosting components into upcoming fragrances. If a scent trend could sum up 2023, it's this.
