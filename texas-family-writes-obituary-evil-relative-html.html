    Don't speak ill of the dead, unless they REALLY deserve it.  

    A Texas family got uncomfortably 'real' in their scathing obituary for Leslie Ray Charping. 

    Charping died on January 30, 2017, at the age of 75, and his family ran a brutally honest obituary, eulogizing their 'evil' late relative, reported the New York Post. 

    Scroll down for video 

    It only got worse: the family said his death came at an age that was '29 years longer than expected and much longer than he deserved'

    'Leslie was surprisingly intelligent, however he lacked ambition to do anything more than being reckless, wasteful, squandering the family savings and fantasizing about get rich quick schemes

    They wrote that he 'leaves behind two relieved children' and 'countless other victims including an ex wife, relatives, friends, neighbors, doctors, nurses and random strangers.' 

    And it only got worse. The family said his death came at an age that was '29 years longer than expected and much longer than he deserved.'

    'At a young age, Leslie quickly became a model example of bad parenting combined with mental illness and a complete commitment to drinking, drugs, womanizing and being generally offensive.'  

    Roy Charping, pictured, is one of the two children that were left behind by the deceased 

    The family is not holding a funeral service. His daughter, Sheila Smith, (far left and far right) is pictured with one unidentified women and her husband, and was one of the people to help in writing the obituary

    In typical obituary fashion, it went on to list his hobbies. However, less typical were those listed, which included abusing his family and expediting trips to heaven for his pets. 

    He was also interested in fishing, which 'he was less skilled with than the previously mentioned.'  

    'Leslie was surprisingly intelligent, however he lacked ambition to do anything more than being reckless, wasteful, squandering the family savings and fantasizing about get rich quick schemes. 

    'Leslie's life served no other purpose, he did not contribute to society or serve his community and he possessed no redeeming qualities besides quick [witted] sarcasm, which was amusing during his sober days.'

    It concluded that his passing 'proves that evil does not in fact die and hopefully marks a time of healing and safety for all.'

    The family is not holding a funeral service.
