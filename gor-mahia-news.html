    Gor Mahia Football Club is a Kenyan professional football club based in Nairobi, Kenya. The club was founded on February 17, 1968, and has since become one of the most successful clubs in Kenya.

    The club has won the Kenyan Premier League a record 19 times and has also won the Kenyan Cup 11 times. Gor Mahia is also the only team from Kenya to have won an African continental title, the African Cup Winners’ Cup in 1987.

    Managers

    Gor Mahia has had several managers over the years. Here are some of them:

    • 2019-2021: Steven Polack, England
    • 2018-2019: Hassan Oktay, Turkey
    • 2017-2018: Dylan Kerr, England
    • 2013-2014: Bobby Williamson, Scotland

    Players

    Gor Mahia has had many talented players over the years. Here are some of them:

    • Kenneth Muguna, Midfielder
    • Joash Onyango, Defender
    • Nicholas Kipkirui, Forward
    • Samuel Onyango, Forward

    History

    Gor Mahia was founded in 1968 by two Luo Clubs, Luo Star FC and Luo Union FC. The team was initially a community affair team where all players were from the Luo community.

    The name “Gor Mahia” belongs to the son of Ogada and grandson of Ogallo, a prominent Luo hero born in the late 18th century.
