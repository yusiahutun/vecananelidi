    LIV Golf Hong Kong tickets go on general sale for first Asian stop of 2024 season

    Tickets for LIV Golf Hong Kong have gone on sale to the general public, with organisers targeting fans across the Greater Bay Area. The tournament at Hong Kong Golf Club in March will be the circuits first stop of the season in Asia, with Singapore hosting LIV Golf for the second year running in May.

    18 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Tickets for LIV Golf Hong Kong have gone on sale to the general public, with organisers targeting fans across the Greater Bay Area.

    The tournament at Hong Kong Golf Club in March will be the circuit’s first stop of the season in Asia, with Singapore hosting LIV Golf for the second year running in May.

    A world-class field, including the likes of Jon Rahm, Brooks Koepka and Phil Mickelson, will battle it out around the composite course in Fanling between March 8 and 10.

    And while some players will be seeing the course for the first time, the likes of Ian Poulter, Talor Gooch, Cameron Smith and Patrick Reed have all had recent experiences of a layout Poulter said tested “every aspect of a golfer’s skill”.

    The Majesticks GC’s co-captain has played in the city on numerous occasions, most recently at last year’s World City Championship, and won the Hong Kong Open in 2010, finishing on 22-under-par.

    Smith, meanwhile, was one of 10 LIV golfers who took part in the 2023 edition, finishing second behind Ben Campbell on 18 under.

    Grounds passes for LIV Golf Hong Kong, starting from HK$1,085 are on sale and are all-inclusive of the tournament fan activities on site.

    “For a limited period, until this Monday [January 15], fans can take advantage of a 15 per cent discount on ground passes to LIV Golf Hong Kong,” a LIV spokesman said. “Hospitality packages offering an exclusive tournament experience featuring premium seating, and food and drink offerings are also available and expected to sell out fast.”
